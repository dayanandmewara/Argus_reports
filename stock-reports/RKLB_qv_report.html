Rocket Lab is the only publicly-traded, vertically-integrated Western space company capable of challenging SpaceX across launch, spacecraft manufacturing, and constellation software. With 21 Electron launches in 2025 (100% success rate), a record $1.85B backlog, and the Neutron medium-lift rocket targeting Q4 2026 first flight, the investment thesis is genuinely compelling — but the current price at ~52x forward EV/Revenue prices perfection. FCF remains deeply negative, gross margins at 34% are hardware not software, and Neutron has already slipped once. A WATCH at these levels; entry zone of $45–$55 (30–35x forward EV/Revenue) provides meaningful margin of safety.
Overview — Business & Classification
Business Model

Rocket Lab operates across three integrated verticals: Launch Services (Electron small-lift rocket, HASTE hypersonic vehicle), Space Systems (spacecraft manufacturing, Photon bus, satellite components, solar arrays via SolAero, star trackers, reaction wheels, laser comms via Mynaric), and Software (MAX constellation management, InterMission ground operations). This vertical integration mirrors SpaceX's strategy — control every layer of the value chain to capture margin everywhere.

Revenue mix (FY2025): Space Systems ~68% ($410M), Launch Services ~32% ($192M). The Space Systems segment is becoming the primary growth driver and carries superior margins.

Why This Stock Is in the Series

Rocket Lab is the most important publicly-traded infrastructure play on the New Space economy. It is the only proven commercial launch alternative to SpaceX. The investment thesis is binary-adjacent: if Neutron succeeds, RKLB becomes a space prime competing for the largest government and commercial contracts. If Neutron fails or is severely delayed, RKLB remains a strong but niche small-launch provider with excellent Space Systems margins.

The challenge: at current prices, Neutron success is already substantially priced in. This creates a classic investor dilemma — great business, great long-term vision, wrong entry price.

Category B Rationale

Rocket Lab fits Category B: revenue CAGR >25% (3yr CAGR ~42%), large underpenetrated market (space is early-inning), ROIC growing from deeply negative toward positive, FCF accelerating but still negative due to Neutron investment. This is not Category F (AI-native) — the moat is launch frequency and vertical integration, not an AI data flywheel.

AI Neutral rationale: Rocket Lab uses AI in trajectory optimization, MAX software autonomy, and constellation management — but AI does not change their fundamental competitive position. They are infrastructure for AI satellite constellations, not an AI company themselves. Mynaric (laser comms) is hardware for data downlink, not AI moat.

Launch Frequency Leadership ★★★★

Electron is the world's most frequently launched small orbital rocket. 100% success in 2025. 21 missions. Flight heritage (80+ missions) creates an enormous reliability moat that emerging competitors (Firefly, Relativity) cannot replicate without years of launches. First-mover reliability compounds — customers choose proven, not experimental.

Vertical Integration ★★★★

Rocket Lab manufactures its own Rutherford engines (3D-printed), carbon composite airframes, flight software, reaction wheels, star trackers, solar cells (SolAero), and now laser comms (Mynaric). This mirrors SpaceX's model — control every layer = capture margin everywhere + supply chain independence. No other public space company matches this depth.

US Sovereign Launch Capability ★★★★

Post-Ukraine, US government mandates sovereign launch capability for national security payloads. Rocket Lab (NZ-HQ but US-manufactured and US-launched) is the only non-SpaceX provider with proven US orbital capability. This creates a structural government-mandated demand floor. VICTUS HAZE (24-hour rapid response) proves operational doctrine fit.

Defense Moat via Trust ★★★

Cleared US government supplier. $816M SDA Tranche III prime contract. NSSL Phase 3 Lane 1 candidate (pending Neutron). Government trust is earned over decades — Rocket Lab's NZ origin and transparent operations have passed US national security vetting. This creates deep moat vs foreign competitors.

Neutron Optionality (Unproven) ★★★

If Neutron launches successfully in Q4 2026, Rocket Lab becomes the only SpaceX F9 alternative for medium-lift. This would open the SDA Tranche IV, IV/V constellation deployment, and eventually NSSL Phase 3 Lane 1 contracts — the most lucrative in the industry. Binary event: priced partially in current $82.93.

Software Switching Costs (Early) ★★

MAX constellation management software deployed across 1,700+ missions. Customers building constellations on MAX have meaningful switching costs — re-qualification of ground systems, telemetry interfaces, and operations procedures takes time. InterMission (announced March 2025) deepens this moat. Not yet as sticky as enterprise SaaS, but building toward it.

Guidance Reliability: Revenue guidance has been consistently accurate (Q3 2025 beat was $155M vs $150M consensus). Neutron guidance is consistently optimistic. Bifurcate trust: TRUST revenue guidance; DISCOUNT Neutron schedule.
