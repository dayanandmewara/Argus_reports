Business Overview

Delhivery is India's largest fully-integrated third-party logistics (3PL) provider by revenue, offering express parcel delivery, part-truckload (PTL) and full-truckload (FTL) freight, warehousing, supply-chain software (OS1), cross-border express, and — as of June 2026 — a commercial geospatial AI platform (Delhivery Maps). V Founded in 2011 and headquartered in Gurugram, the company listed on the NSE/BSE in May 2022 raising ₹5,235 Cr. V

The single biggest structural event since IPO is the July 2025 acquisition of a 99.87% stake in Ecom Express, its closest scaled competitor, folding a rival network into Delhivery's own and materially consolidating the Indian express-logistics market. V

Why Category D — Turnaround / Special Situation

Delhivery does not fit a "quality compounder" template today: ROE is 1.87% and ROCE 2.82% V — both far below the 15%+ threshold for Categories A–C. What earns it a Category D slot rather than "wrong framework" (E) is that these metrics are visibly improving off a low base, driven by identifiable, dateable events: EBITDA margin nearly doubled from FY25 to FY26 (3.8% → 7.3% of revenue), free cash flow turned positive for the first time (₹89 Cr in FY26), and the Ecom Express integration removes a major price-competing rival. V This is a classic event-driven re-rating thesis, not a proven moat-and-compounding story yet.

AI Overlay — Why WIDENER

Delhivery's June 2026 launch of Delhivery Maps, built on its proprietary "Naksha LLM" geospatial model trained on telemetry from over 2 billion shipments and ~1 billion daily GPS pings across 100,000+ vehicles, is a genuine attempt to convert operational data exhaust into a moat and a new revenue line. V It already replaces third-party mapping across 100% of Delhivery's own network and is now sold externally to e-commerce, quick-commerce and ride-hailing platforms. V This lowers Delhivery's own unit routing cost and opens a asset-light software/API revenue stream — the textbook definition of an AI Widener — though the external flywheel loop (more third-party users → more data → better model → more users) is only weeks old and unproven at scale, so we grade the Data Flywheel FORMING rather than CONFIRMED (see Tab 3 and Tab 9).

Macro Relevance

Delhivery sits at the intersection of two macro tailwinds and one macro headwind: India's e-commerce logistics market is forecast to grow at a wide range of ~9–26% CAGR depending on methodology/scope E, tier-3-and-below cities already account for ~40% of e-commerce logistics volume signalling deep penetration runway E, while intensifying competition from Amazon's own logistics arm, Xpressbees, Shadowfax and others caps pricing power.
