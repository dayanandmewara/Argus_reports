Category & Series Rationale

Category D — Turnaround / Special Situation because Borosil Renewables has reported net losses in FY24 (₹50 Cr), FY25 (₹87 Cr), and FY26 (₹70 Cr reported, including ₹213–326 Cr in German subsidiary exceptional charges). The underlying India business has undergone a structural inflection: anti-dumping duties imposed in December 2024 on Chinese and Vietnamese solar glass imports (₹570–664/ton) instantly collapsed the price competition and drove standalone EBITDA margins from 4% to 32% in a single year — the fastest margin recovery in the company's history.


Why analyse now? Borosil posted its best-ever standalone EBITDA (₹492 Cr, +172% YoY) in FY26. India's solar installations hit a record 44.6 GW in FY26 (nearly double FY25), and ALMM-II mandates from June 2026 will further lock domestic solar glass demand. The company is mid-way through a ₹950 Cr capacity expansion (1,000→1,600 TPD), expected by December 2026. However, the stock at ₹534 trades above our Cat D MoS entry zone (₹290–390) — making this a monitoring candidate rather than an immediate buy.
