Business Overview

Est. 1993 · HQ Mumbai · ~3,400 employees

Nuvama Wealth Management Ltd (formerly Edelweiss Securities Ltd, renamed August 2022) is the demerged wealth-management arm of the erstwhile Edelweiss Group, listed independently since the demerger became effective in May 2023. The controlling shareholder is PAGAC Ecstasy Pte Ltd, an entity of Pacific Alliance Group (PAG), a large Asia-focused alternative investment manager that acquired control in FY2020-21.

The business runs four integrated segments: Wealth Management (the largest and fastest-growing, split between "Nuvama Wealth" for affluent/HNI clients and "Nuvama Private" for UHNI families and family offices), Asset Management (AIFs and PMS across public markets and commercial real estate, run with a Cushman & Wakefield JV), Capital Markets (institutional broking, investment banking, merchant banking, fixed income) and Asset Services (custody and clearing for institutional clients — record profits in FY26).

Scale as of March 2026: total client assets of ₹4,52,548 Cr (+5% YoY) across 1.3 million affluent/HNI clients and 4,750+ UHNI families, served by roughly 1,250 relationship managers from 100+ offices. A Dubai (DIFC) subsidiary launched in FY25 extends the platform to offshore private wealth.

Why this stock, this cycle

India's HNI/UHNI population is compounding faster than the broader economy, and wealth management remains structurally under-penetrated relative to developed markets — Bernstein has likened the sector's current stage to private banking's early rise in India in the late 1990s. Nuvama sits among the top-3 listed pure-play platforms addressing that pool, with an integrated model (wealth + AMC + capital markets + custody) that few peers replicate end-to-end.

What makes this one hard, not easy

The growth story is real but has visibly decelerated (FY26 revenue +8% vs FY25's +41%), the balance sheet carries real leverage from the margin-funding book, FY26 operating cash flow went negative on working-capital swings, and — the standout concern — 62.8% of the promoter's own stake is pledged. None of this makes the business bad; it makes today's price a much harder bar to clear.

Moat Sources

Integrated platform / switching costs

UHNI and family-office relationships (Nuvama Private) are advisory-intensive and multi-year — once tax, estate and cross-border structuring is embedded with a firm, switching is costly and disruptive for the client. This is the strongest leg of the moat. E

Scale & brand within a narrow field

₹4.5 trillion in client assets and a top-3 position among listed pure-play wealth platforms is a real scale advantage in a field with a limited number of credible competitors at this size. V

Regulatory / licensing barriers

SEBI broking, PMS, AIF, depository-participant and merchant-banking licenses represent real (if not insurmountable) barriers to a new entrant replicating the full integrated model. E

Institutional promoter capacity

PAG brings capital and M&A capability (e.g. the Cushman & Wakefield JV, Dubai expansion) that a standalone founder-led peer would find harder to fund. Cuts both ways — see Management tab on the pledge. E

ROIC as Moat Evidence

ROE of 24–31% across FY24–FY26 sits inside the framework's "wide moat" band (20–40% ROIC) — genuine value creation, not a boundary case. It is not in the >40% "exceptional/unassailable" band, which is consistent with a real but contestable moat rather than a structural monopoly.

Macro Stress Test

The single biggest structural vulnerability: a large share of revenue (ARR, brokerage, AUM-linked fees) scales with equity-market levels and transaction activity. A sharp Nifty correction would compress fee income across Wealth, Capital Markets and Asset Management simultaneously — this isn't a diversified-away risk, it's a shared macro factor across all four segments. FY26's own deceleration (+8% vs +41% in FY25) is a mild preview of what a harder market slowdown could do to the growth line.

Data Flywheel Check

Q1 — Proprietary data no one else has? Client portfolio and transaction data is real but not obviously more proprietary than what 360 ONE, Anand Rathi or bank private-wealth arms hold on their own books. E
Q2 — Does the product improve as more clients use it? No disclosed evidence of AI/advisory quality compounding with client-base growth.
Q3 — Does that improvement attract more clients? Not evidenced — client growth appears driven by RM relationships and market conditions, not a product flywheel.

Verdict: FEATURE — AI/data tooling adds real value (RM productivity) but doesn't yet compound into a self-reinforcing moat. Full AI scoring in Tab 9.

Concerns — Led by the Promoter Pledge

RED FLAG Promoter pledge: 62.8% of promoter holding (Mar 2026)

Independently corroborated across four sources (Screener.in, MarketsMojo, and two data aggregators). This mechanically trips the framework's own governance rule: pledges >40% of promoter holding = AVOID-level trigger. VERIFIED (figure) UNVERIFIED (specific purpose)

Context that matters before treating this as disqualifying: PAG is an institutional PE-style promoter, and a pledge this size is more consistent with acquisition-financing collateral (shares pledged against the debt used to fund the original controlling-stake purchase) than with the individual-promoter distress pattern the rule was designed to catch. That said, none of the sources reviewed confirm the specific purpose — this is a real, unresolved gap, not a resolved non-issue, and it's the single biggest reason this report doesn't go above WATCH.

Regulatory Matter — SEBI Front-Running Case

Nuvama is named — as a facilitating broker, not an accused party — in a SEBI case against Ketan Parekh, Rohit Salgaocar and Ashok Kumar Poddar over front-running of a large FPI client's trades (originally a Jan-2025 interim order; proceedings, including show-cause notices to Capital Group entities, continued into mid-2026). SEBI's own order explicitly states no evidence was found implicating Nuvama or Motilal Oswal in the front-running itself. The scrutiny centred on a referral/revenue-sharing arrangement (~₹19 Cr in commissions Nuvama paid to Salgaocar's firm) that SEBI linked to the information leak. Separately, Bernstein flagged this matter as a source of near-term revenue headwind from one large affected client. V

Reverse-DCF: what CMP requires you to believe

Holding growth at Base case, ₹1,809 requires an exit multiple of roughly 28.7x — below Nuvama's own current multiple and well below 360 ONE's, so not an unreasonable ask by itself. Alternatively, holding the exit multiple at 19x, CMP requires Stage-1 growth of roughly 31.7% — above FY26's actual +8% and above the 5-year CAGR of 26%, though below FY25's own +41%. ESTIMATED

A defensible "both move a bit" case

18% growth (above Base, below the 5yr CAGR) combined with a 28x exit multiple (below Nuvama's own current 31.4x) produces an IV of ₹1,906 — about 5% above CMP. Under that lens the stock isn't dramatically rich; it's close to fair. ESTIMATED

Why the verdict in Tab 6 still leads with the more conservative number: a margin-of-safety framework deliberately avoids assuming today's rich multiple persists into the terminal year — assuming your exit multiple equals today's entry multiple is exactly the mechanism behind multiple-compression losses, and several richly-multiple Indian financial compounders have de-rated hard in past cycles with no change in the underlying business. The Cat-C-anchored WATCH★★ verdict is this framework's disciplined answer to "is there a margin of safety on conservative assumptions" — and on that specific question, the answer is no. Whether Indian wealth-management multiples structurally hold near 25–35x for the next 5–7 years, as 360 ONE's and Nuvama's own trading history suggest they might, or mean-revert toward 18–22x, is a forecasting judgment, not a settled fact. On the more growth-friendly view, this stock is close to fairly priced today rather than ~90% rich. Both views are shown here rather than only the one the framework's default assumptions produce.

FINAL VERDICT

WATCH ★★

Decent-to-good quality with real, specific concerns — not a business to write off, but not a business to buy at ₹1,809 either. Genuine structural tailwind, a top-3 integrated platform, and a fresh credit-rating upgrade are weighed against a sharply decelerated FY26, negative operating cash flow, and — the deciding factor — a 62.8% promoter pledge that remains unexplained in the sources reviewed. Price has moved further from every fundamentals-based intrinsic value since the 12 May report (₹1,735 → ₹1,809), while the blended IV estimate itself has come down slightly on more conservative post-FY26 growth assumptions — widening the gap from ~58% to ~90%. That combination is enough to take one star off the prior WATCH★★★, not enough (given no confirmed fraud and a partly-explicable pledge structure) to drop all the way to PASS.

Important conditionality: this verdict rests on a conservative, mean-reversion exit multiple (Cat-C generic band, 18–22x) rather than the richer multiples the sector currently trades at. See Tab 5 → Growth & Multiple Sensitivity — under a defensible growth-friendly view (18% growth, 28x exit multiple, both below Nuvama's own historical extremes), fair value lands within ~5% of CMP, not ~90% below it. Whether Indian wealth-management multiples mean-revert or structurally persist is a judgment call this report doesn't resolve for you.
