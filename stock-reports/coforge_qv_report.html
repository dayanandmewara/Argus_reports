Business Overview Coforge Limited — NSE: COFORGE
🏢 Company at a Glance

Coforge (formerly NIIT Technologies) is a global IT engineering and digital services company headquartered in Noida, India. Founded in 1992, it has evolved from a generalist IT services firm into a hyper-specialised, domain-deep engineering powerhouse. The company operates across 21 countries with 35,777 employees and serves over 250 global clients including British Airways, ING Group, SEI Investments, Sabre, and SITA.

Private equity firm EQT Partners (successor to Baring PE) controls Coforge with zero traditional promoter holding. Under CEO Sudhir Singh, the company has undergone a dramatic transformation — from a mid-tier generalist to a focused, AI-led engineering leader with deep BFSI, Travel, and Insurance verticals. The Cigniti Technologies acquisition ($220M, Dec 2024) expanded into healthcare, retail, and hi-tech verticals.

⭐ Why This Stock Is in the Series

Coforge represents a high-risk, high-reward bet on India's IT sector transformation. It is the fastest-growing mid-tier Indian IT company by revenue (FY26: +35.9%), yet its stock fell 50% from peak — creating what may be a rare value-meets-growth entry point. The investment thesis rests on four pillars: (1) exceptional domain depth in BFSI and Travel — high-switching-cost verticals, (2) margin expansion story (EBIT from 11% to 14.4% in FY26, guidance for 20.5%+ EBITDA in FY27), (3) FCF inflection — guidance for 100%+ FCF/PAT conversion from FY27, and (4) $1.75B order book providing 12+ months revenue visibility. The key risk is AI disruption of traditional IT services labour arbitrage models over a 3–5 year horizon.

🌀 Data Flywheel Assessment — Mandatory for All Stocks
AI FEATURE, NOT MOAT ★
✗
Q1: Proprietary training data no competitor can access?
NO. Coforge's AI tools (CodeInsight.AI, Quasar GenAI) run on client data that legally belongs to clients. Coforge does not retain or train on proprietary outcome data from client engagements. AI platforms built on GitHub Copilot / OpenAI APIs provide no exclusive data advantage.
✗
Q2: Does AI measurably improve with more users/data?
NO confirmed evidence. Coforge trains 10,000+ developers on GitHub Copilot, which improves Microsoft's AI, not Coforge's. CodeInsight.AI helps Coforge delivery but is not a self-improving flywheel creating a competitive moat vs. peers using the same tools.
✗
Q3: Does better AI attract more clients creating a self-reinforcing loop?
NOT YET. Coforge wins deals on domain expertise + relationship depth + delivery excellence — not on a proprietary AI model that differentiates from peers. The AI tools provide cost efficiency (6a lever) but not a moat-building feedback loop.
Flywheel Score: 1/10 — Data quality: 0/3 | Improvement evidence: 1/4 | Loop speed: 0/3
Conclusion: AI adds value as a cost efficiency tool but does NOT compound Coforge's competitive defensibility. The moat grade is NOT upgraded.
Moat Sources
🔒 Switching Costs (BFSI & Travel Domain)
★★★★☆

Coforge manages mission-critical systems for global airlines (10 of top 15), global banks, and insurance carriers. Replacing a Coforge system means migrating core banking, reservation systems, or claims processing — typically 2–4 year transitions at enormous risk and cost. Client stickiness is evidenced by expanding large account revenues (Cigniti top 2 clients: $25M → $75M per annum post-integration). MOAT STATUS: HOLDS even in stress scenario.

🧠 Domain Expertise (BFSI/Travel IP)
★★★☆☆

Coforge has built deep domain libraries, frameworks, and accelerators for BFSI (Duck Creek platform leadership — "Leader and Star Performer" Everest Group 2026), Travel (SITA, Sabre integrations), and Insurance. These IP assets reduce delivery time and increase quality vs. generalist IT firms. However, AI coding tools are beginning to narrow the IP advantage — junior BFSI IT code can increasingly be generated by foundation models, threatening the entry-level labour component. MOAT STATUS: WEAKENS slowly over 5–7 years as AI matures.

🤝 Client Relationships & Trust
★★★★☆

Over 250 global clients; many multi-decade relationships. British Airways, ING Group, SEI Investments are long-tenure accounts. High repeat business = structural revenue visibility. Top 10 clients at 35% revenue with expanding wallet share. MOAT STATUS: HOLDS — trust is hard to replicate.

📈 Efficient Scale / Global Delivery
★★★☆☆

35,777 employees across 21 countries; 26 delivery centres. Gives near-shore + offshore blended delivery cost advantage vs. pure offshore or pure onshore competitors. MOAT STATUS: HOLDS in base scenario; AI gradually erodes the 'more people = more capacity' benefit.
