Overview & Series Rationale
What Vertiv Does

Vertiv designs, manufactures, and services power management, cooling, and IT infrastructure for data centers, communications networks, and industrial environments across 130+ countries. Core products: UPS systems, switchgear, busway, thermal management (air and liquid cooling), racks, and lifecycle services. As AI drives rack power densities from 10 kW (traditional) to 120–150 kW (AI GPU), Vertiv's liquid-cooling portfolio has shifted from optional upgrade to non-negotiable infrastructure. The company is not an AI software play — it is the physical plumbing and power rails without which AI chips cannot operate.

Moat Threats & Competitor Assessment
Schneider Electric (SE) — Biggest Rival
Schneider competes across UPS and cooling. Capable but lacks Vertiv's liquid cooling IP depth and NVIDIA-aligned roadmap. Cannot match Vertiv's near-term capacity commitments. Vertiv advantages: speed, depth, NVIDIA lock-in.
Eaton Corporation (ETN) — Power Focus
Eaton is strong in power management but has minimal liquid cooling portfolio. Competes on UPS/switchgear but not on thermal management at rack level. Different buyer segment (facilities vs. IT).
Microsoft Microfluidics — Emerging Tech Risk
Microsoft announced proprietary microfluidics cooling technology. If hyperscalers develop in-house cooling tech (particularly for their own chip designs), this could reduce Vertiv TAM for specific GPU configurations. Medium-term monitoring required.
CoolIT Systems / Asetek — CDU Specialists
Smaller CDU specialists. Competition at component level but not at system-integration level. Vertiv can partner (as they have with CoolIT) or acquire to maintain technology leadership.
