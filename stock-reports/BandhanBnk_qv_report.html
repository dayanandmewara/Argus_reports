Business Overview

The Bandhan Story

Founded as a microfinance institution in 2001 by Chandra Shekhar Ghosh, Bandhan became India's first MFI to receive a universal banking licence in 2015 — a regulatory landmark. The bank listed via IPO in 2018 (subscribed 15×) and became the 8th most-valued bank in India on listing day. Its thesis was compelling: channel the trust and collection discipline of microfinance into a full-service bank serving Eastern India's underbanked population.

The model worked brilliantly until COVID (FY21) when joint-liability group lending collapsed. Then came Assam and West Bengal election cycles, which historically depress MFI repayments as political loan-waiver narratives spread. The bank has been navigating this since FY22, with GNPA peaking above 10% in the MFI book at its worst. Bandhan 2.0 — the strategic restructuring launched under founder-CEO Chandra Shekhar Ghosh and now being executed by incoming CEO Partha Pratim Sengupta — pivots the bank toward secured retail, housing finance, and SME lending while retaining its low-cost deposit franchise in Bengal.

The central investment question for March 2026: Is the turnaround real and durable, or is GNPA improvement partly a function of ARC sales masking structural weakness?

Why This Stock Is In The Series

Bandhan presents the classic Buffett dilemma: a genuinely wide moat (Eastern India deposit franchise + 25M+ microfinance customer relationships) currently obscured by cyclical NPA stress and a management transition. The stock trades at ~1.0× book — near the cheapest it has ever been in its listed history. Yet the promoter overhang (mandatory regulatory reduction from 39.7% to 26% by 2030) acts as a structural ceiling on near-term price appreciation.

The AI angle is genuinely interesting. Bandhan possesses 20+ years of granular behavioural repayment data on India's most financially excluded population — data that no credit bureau fully captures. This is a potential data flywheel for AI-driven credit scoring that could expand its serviceable market by 3–5× while reducing credit costs. The question is execution speed.

Economic Moat Assessment

Moat Sources — Banking Overlay

Deposit Franchise — Eastern India
★★★★
6,300+ outlets, 3.44 Cr customers, deep penetration in West Bengal, Assam, Bihar, Odisha. Strong brand recall among financially excluded segments. Deposit CAGR 12%+ sustained. This is the single strongest moat — the ability to raise cheap retail deposits in semi-urban and rural India is rare and hard to replicate.
MFI Trust Network (20+ years)
★★★★
Joint-liability group (JLG) lending structure creates social collateral that formal banks cannot replicate. 25M+ MFI customer relationships with 20+ years of behavioural repayment data. This trust cannot be digitally disintermediated in the short term.
CASA Switching Cost
★★★
Rural and semi-urban customers with Bandhan accounts face high switching friction (distance, familiarity, no alternative nearby). However, CASA ratio declining from 40% → 27% is eroding this advantage as term deposits compete. Moat weakening.
Regulatory Franchise (Universal Bank)
★★★
Universal banking licence is not easily awarded. Bandhan's status as India's first MFI-to-universal bank is a regulatory moat. Competitors cannot replicate this conversion pathway.
Data Moat (MFI Repayment History)
★★★
20+ years of granular weekly repayment data on India's most excluded borrowers. No credit bureau has equivalent depth on these segments. AI training potential is significant. Flywheel FORMING — not yet confirmed with metrics.
Geographic Concentration Risk
⚠ RISK
58%+ of EEB book in Eastern India — politically sensitive, election-cycle sensitive. This is not a moat but a concentration risk that recurrently impairs the moat's earning power. Bandhan 2.0 is working to diversify (non-East now 55% of outlets).

FORMING — NOT YET CONFIRMED WITH METRICS. The Salesforce Agentforce LOS deployment (March 2025) is designed to improve credit evaluation accuracy with usage. This question cannot be marked CONFIRMED without a quantitative metric from management. ESTIMATED — monitoring needed

POTENTIAL — NOT YET ACTIVE. The loop would be: better AI credit scoring → approve more previously unscored borrowers → those borrowers repay or default → data improves → model accuracy rises → bank can lend more. This loop takes 2–3 years from LOS deployment to show in data. Too early to confirm. UNVERIFIED

AI Management — Specific Assessment

AI Team Quality: COO Ratan Kumar Kesh is the AI/technology champion. Salesforce partnership (March 2025) is credible — Agentforce is production-grade AI for loan origination. Core banking upgrade (October 2023) provides the data infrastructure for AI training. However, there is no evidence of dedicated AI research team, ML publications, or proprietary model building. Bandhan is an AI user, not an AI builder.

Capital Allocation for AI: Technology capex appears appropriate — outsourcing AI to Salesforce (best-in-class LOS) while focusing internal resources on domain expertise. This is pragmatic for a bank of this size. Estimated AI/tech spend ~3–5% of revenue (not disclosed formally).

AI Moat Assessment: Bandhan is building AI capability on top of its existing moat rather than building a proprietary AI moat. This is the correct approach for a Level 5 (AI-enabled incumbent). The data flywheel potential is real but the execution is in early stages. Management credibility on AI is MARGINAL → improving.

The Long-Term AI Thesis for Bandhan

Here is the genuine long-term bull case that the market is not pricing: Bandhan possesses the world's most valuable credit-training dataset for India's financially excluded population — 500M+ individual loan repayment observations on borrowers that no credit bureau tracks. If this data is properly trained using modern ML/AI (and Salesforce Agentforce is a step toward this), Bandhan could achieve two transformational outcomes:

1. TAM Expansion: India has ~190M adults with no credit score. AI scoring using Bandhan's data could make 30–50M of these bankable for the first time. At ₹50,000 average loan size, this is a ₹15–25 lakh crore TAM expansion — roughly 10–20× Bandhan's current loan book.

2. Credit Cost Reduction: Better AI prediction of default → lower provisioning requirements → higher RoA. A 50bps improvement in credit cost (from ~2.5% to ~2.0% of loans) would add ~₹700 Cr to annual PAT — equivalent to a 25% PAT boost on FY27E base.

Neither outcome is priced at 1.0× book. The market is pricing peak-provisioning FY26 and ignoring the Bandhan 2.0 + AI transformation thesis entirely. This is where the asymmetric opportunity lies — but it requires 5–7 year patience and conviction in management execution.
