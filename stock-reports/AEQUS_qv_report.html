Company Overview
Business Identity

Aequs Limited is India's only precision component manufacturer operating a fully vertically integrated aerospace ecosystem within a single SEZ — spanning forging, machining, surface treatment, and assembly under one roof in Belagavi, Karnataka. Founded in 2000 (originally as Quest Machining), it has evolved over 25 years into a Tier-1 certified supplier to global aerospace OEMs. VERIFIED


The company listed on NSE/BSE in December 2025 via IPO at ₹124/share, raising ₹670 Cr fresh capital primarily for debt reduction and capacity expansion. The IPO was subscribed 101.6× — reflecting institutional conviction in the structural aerospace thesis.

Two Business Segments

Aerospace (88% revenue) — Precision machined and forged components for engine systems, landing gear, structures, cargo interiors, assemblies. Clients: Airbus, Boeing, Safran, Collins Aerospace, Spirit AeroSystems, Honeywell, Eaton, GKN, Bombardier. 5,200+ SKUs across A320, A350, B737, B787 programmes. Average client tenure: 15 years. VERIFIED

Consumer (12% revenue) — Engineered plastics, consumer electronics components, toys, non-stick cookware, small appliances. Clients: Hasbro, Spinmaster, Wonderchef, Tramontina. This segment is currently loss-making and a key drag on consolidated financials.

Why This Stock Is In The Series

Three structural reasons make Aequs a must-watch for long-horizon investors: (1) China+1 / Europe+1 shift — global aerospace OEMs actively reducing China and European supply chain dependency; India's cost-quality-certifiable ecosystem is the primary alternative. Airbus has publicly committed to increasing India sourcing from $1.4B (FY25) to $2B by 2030. VERIFIED (2) India's commercial aviation supercycle — IndiGo, Air India and others have placed 1,100+ aircraft orders; component demand has a 10–15yr backlog. (3) Irreplicable infrastructure moat — Aequs's SEZ took 25 years and hundreds of millions in capital to build; Nadcap certifications alone take 5–7 years to earn. A competitor cannot replicate this overnight. The company is entering its operating leverage inflection year (Q3 FY26: revenue +51% YoY, EBITDA +353% YoY). The losses are structural not permanent — they are the price of building a platform. But the consumer segment needs to turn, FCF needs to materialise, and debt needs to continue falling. This is a turnaround with a quality franchise underneath.

Framework Classification Note
Why Category D (Turnaround)

Aequs fails standard quality tests: net losses for 3 consecutive years, negative ROE (-11%), ROCE barely above zero (1.1%), negative FCF historically, bloating inventory (420 days) and debtor days (62). Under the Buffett QV framework, these metrics classify it as a turnaround, not a quality compounder — yet. The underlying aerospace franchise is quality-grade; the business has not yet demonstrated that quality in reported numbers. The task is to identify at what price the margin of safety compensates for this turnaround risk.

Why AI NEUTRAL ⚪

Precision manufacturing is a materials, metrology and certification business — not an AI-first business. Aequs uses technology (CNC, ERP, quality management systems) extensively, but there is no evidence of a proprietary AI model, data flywheel, or AI-driven competitive advantage. The company has entered a UAV JV (Ajna Aerospace, Mar 2026, ₹10 Cr, 33.3% stake) which is a nascent AI-adjacent play, but too early to rerate. AI NEUTRAL is the correct classification.

Economic Moat Analysis
Moat Source 1 — Vertically Integrated SEZ Infrastructure
★★★★★

The Belagavi SEZ is the only facility in India combining forging, machining, surface treatment, aerostructure assembly, and prototyping under one roof with aerospace certification. A competitor would need 10–15 years and ₹1,500+ Cr to replicate this. Physical infrastructure moats in aerospace are durable because customers want proven, certified suppliers — switching is operationally disruptive and safety-critical.

Moat Source 2 — Nadcap & OEM Certifications
★★★★★

Nadcap certification (aerospace special processes) is the hardest-to-earn quality badge in manufacturing. Combined with direct Airbus and Boeing supplier approvals, these certifications create a 5–7yr barrier for any new entrant. Aequs has received the Airbus Detailed Parts Partner (D2P) Award 6 times since 2016. These certifications are non-transferable — they attach to the facility, not the company's name.

Moat Source 3 — Long-Term OEM Relationships
★★★★★

Average 15-year relationship tenure with top 3 customer groups. Top 5 customers = 66% revenue (H1 FY26). While this is also a concentration risk, the longevity proves switching cost. Boeing and Airbus do not change approved suppliers casually — the qualification process is expensive for both parties. This is a sticky revenue moat, albeit with customer concentration risk.

Moat Source 4 — Product Portfolio Breadth (5,200+ SKUs)
★★★★★

The 5,200+ approved aerospace products across every major commercial programme creates a portfolio depth that is essentially a library of certified intellectual property. Competitors entering the space must build this library part by part, programme by programme — a decade-long process. This is an invisible but highly durable moat.

Data Flywheel Assessment
⚙ AI FEATURE — NOT A MOAT
2/10

Q1 — Proprietary Training Data: Aequs has proprietary manufacturing process data, quality inspection records, and 25 years of precision manufacturing know-how. However, this data is not used to train AI models — it informs process engineers and quality systems (ERP, MES). Not an AI data asset in the modern sense. Not confirmed

Q2 — Does AI improve with more data/users? No active evidence of an AI learning loop in manufacturing operations. CNC machines, surface treatment baths, and forging presses improve through human process engineering, not ML feedback loops. Not confirmed

Q3 — Does better AI attract more users? Not applicable — Aequs competes on certification, quality, and delivery reliability, not AI capability. Not confirmed

Verdict: Precision manufacturing is a materials + metrology + human skill business. The UAV JV (Ajna Aerospace) could become an AI-adjacent asset in 3–5 years, but today it's a ₹10 Cr minority stake — not material. AI NEUTRAL classification confirmed.
