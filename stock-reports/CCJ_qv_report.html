Business Overview

Cameco Corporation (TSX: CCO; NYSE: CCJ) is one of the world's largest uranium producers, headquartered in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, Canada. Founded in 1988, Cameco controls the world's two most strategically important uranium mines — McArthur River (world's largest high-grade deposit, 25,000 MT uranium) and Cigar Lake (world's highest-grade at 14.4% vs 0.1% global average). In November 2023, Cameco completed the transformative acquisition of a 49% stake in Westinghouse Electric Company, the nuclear reactor OEM and services leader, partnered with Brookfield Asset Management (51%).

The company operates across three reportable segments: (1) Uranium — exploration, mining, milling, purchase and sale of uranium concentrate; (2) Fuel Services — refining, conversion and fabrication of uranium; and (3) Westinghouse — nuclear reactor technology OEM (AP1000 Gen III+ reactor), plus aftermarket maintenance and services for commercial and government nuclear operators globally. This integration positions Cameco uniquely across the entire nuclear fuel and reactor life cycle. VERIFIED

Why Interesting / Why Challenging
The Compelling Case

Nuclear is undergoing its first structural renaissance in 40 years, with bipartisan US policy support, global reactor new-build (WNA projects 746 GWe by 2040 vs 398 GWe today), and AI data centers creating entirely new demand. Cameco holds the world's two best uranium assets, has a profitable long-term contract book, and controls 49% of Westinghouse — whose AP1000 reactor is the cornerstone of the US nuclear renaissance. The management team has 35+ years of industry expertise and made the disciplined decision to shut McArthur River from 2018–2022 to defend uranium pricing. The Westinghouse acquisition, completed in Nov 2023, was timed to perfection.

The Challenge

Uranium remains partially commodity-driven — spot price volatility can compress margins rapidly (as seen in 2012–2020). WEC's purchase accounting (~CAD $4.2B goodwill) materially suppresses reported GAAP ROE (9.6%) and ROIC (6.5%), making the stock look expensive on headline metrics. At P/E ~95x TTM and P/E ~84x forward, the stock is pricing in a significant portion of the nuclear renaissance bull case already. The current price of $106 USD is 12–28% above the base-case MoS entry zone of $82–95, requiring either a 10–20% correction or confirmation of accelerated nuclear build-out for an optimal entry.

Economic Moat Assessment
★★★★★

World-Class Uranium Reserves (McArthur River & Cigar Lake)

McArthur River is the world's largest uranium deposit (25,000+ MT U). Cigar Lake is the world's highest-grade mine at 14.4% U₃O₈ vs a global average of ~0.1% — over 140× higher grade. Cameco recently increased its Cigar Lake stake to 57.4% (June 1, 2026, acquiring TEPCO's 5%). These assets have 40–60+ year mine lives and cannot be replicated. The grade advantage delivers a massive cost advantage: production cost ~$18–22/lb vs spot $87/lb = ~80% margin at spot prices. Stress test: STRENGTHENS — at $65/lb uranium, these are still the only profitable mines globally.

★★★★★

Westinghouse AP1000 — Nuclear Reactor Technology OEM

Westinghouse's AP1000 is the only fully-licensed Gen III+ pressurized water reactor globally. 8 AP1000 reactors are currently operating (China, UAE), with 4 under construction and 20+ in near-term pipeline including the US "ADVANCE Act" $80B pipeline. AP1000 reactors create a 60+ year service relationship: Cameco/WEC become fuel supplier, maintenance provider, and parts supplier for the reactor's entire life. The 49% WEC stake creates direct equity alignment between Cameco's fuel sales and the reactor OEM that drives demand for that fuel. Stress test: STRENGTHENS — the AP1000 moat deepens as more reactors operate.

★★★★

Long-Term Contract Book — Predictable Revenue at Premium Prices

Over 70% of Cameco's uranium production is sold under long-term contracts extending 7–12 years at prices of $80–86/lb — significantly above historical averages ($40–60/lb in 2015–2020). These contracts provide revenue predictability and insulate from spot price volatility. Nuclear utilities have inelastic demand (fuel = ~5–10% of electricity cost) and cannot easily switch suppliers. Switching would require regulatory re-approval of fuel fabrication specifications — creating structural lock-in. New contracts being signed at even higher floor prices. Stress test: HOLDS — even if spot price collapses to $55, LTC revenue is secured at $80–86.

★★★★

Geopolitical Premium — Canadian Jurisdiction in a Supply-Disrupted World

Post-2022, Western nuclear utilities actively seek to de-risk supply chains from Russia (Rosatom: ~20% of global enrichment) and Kazakhstan (Kazatomprom: ~43% of global production). Canadian supply = safest geopolitical profile. US DOE has established a Uranium Reserve program explicitly to secure domestic and allied supply. Cameco is the single largest supplier in stable western jurisdictions, with no sanctioned-country exposure. This geopolitical moat is widening, not narrowing. Stress test: STRENGTHENS — geopolitical stress makes Cameco more valuable, not less.

★★★

Nuclear Regulatory Expertise & Licensing Barriers

35+ years of CNSC (Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission) compliance and operational track record. Environmental licensing, tailings management, and indigenous community relations in Saskatchewan are 10–20 year barriers to entry. No new tier-one uranium mines have been permitted in North America since Cigar Lake. This creates a moat not of technology but of regulatory trust and permitted capacity. Stress test: HOLDS — regulatory barriers are cyclical-independent.
