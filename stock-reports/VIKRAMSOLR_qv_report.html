Business Overview

Vikram Solar Ltd is one of India's largest pure-play solar photovoltaic module manufacturers, incorporated in 2005 and headquartered in Kolkata, West Bengal. The company designs, manufactures, and markets solar PV modules under brands including Suryava, Hypersol, Paradea, SOMERA, and PREXOS. It also offers EPC (Engineering, Procurement & Construction) services and O&M (Operations & Maintenance) solutions for utility-scale solar projects. VERIFIED

After listing on NSE/BSE in 2025 following an IPO, Vikram Solar now operates 9.5 GW of module manufacturing capacity across three facilities: Falta (West Bengal), Oragadam (Tamil Nadu) and the newly commissioned 5 GW Vallam plant (Tamil Nadu). The company has deployed 10 GW of modules cumulatively across 39 countries — doubling from 5 GW to 10 GW in just two years. VERIFIED

Economic Moat Assessment — Pillar 1
Scale & Cost Advantage (PLI-enabled) ★★★★

Vikram Solar's 9.5 GW module capacity places it among India's top 3 pure-play manufacturers. PLI eligibility for its 2.4 GW TOPCon facility provides a government-backed cost subsidy unavailable to smaller competitors. The ALMM (Approved List) requirement for government-funded projects creates a regulatory moat — only ALMM-listed manufacturers can participate in India's massive public solar tenders. This is a durable near-term advantage, though not permanent. VERIFIED

Technology Leadership (N-TOPCon → HJT) ★★★

Vikram's Vallam facility is built for N-TOPCon (current gold standard) with engineered upgrade paths to HJT (next generation). PVEL Top Performer recognition for multiple consecutive years and Bloomberg NEF Tier 1 status (8 consecutive quarters) creates bankability premium with large institutional buyers. HJT modules command 5–8% price premium. Technology leadership is real but potentially temporary — Chinese manufacturers will close this gap within 18–24 months. VERIFIED

Order Book Visibility & Customer Relationships ★★★

8.2 GW order book (87% domestic, 13% export) provides ~2.5 years of revenue visibility at current run rate. Customer diversification improved dramatically: top 5 customer concentration fell from 80% FY25 to 47% FY26 — a significant de-risk. Key customers include IPPs (69% of order book), C&I (13%), EPCs (11%), and government (7%). NTPC Green Energy, Indian Oil-NTPC JV are recent wins. VERIFIED

Backward Integration Roadmap ★★★

The most strategically important moat-building initiative. India's solar manufacturers currently import ~80% of polysilicon, wafers, and ingots — primarily from China. Vikram's approved ₹3,726 Cr capex for 6 GW wafer-ingot at Gangaikondan (target: FY29) alongside 9 GW cell plant (Dec 2026) will transform cost structure: fully integrated manufacturing should reduce module cost by 15–20% vs module-only peers. This is a wide-moat generator IF execution succeeds. Currently: FORMING. ESTIMATED

Brand & Global Certification (Bankability) ★★★

Bankability is the hidden moat in solar manufacturing. Project finance lenders (banks, infra funds) only accept modules from PVEL Top Performer / Bloomberg NEF Tier 1 manufacturers. Vikram has maintained this status for years — creating a sticky relationship with large institutional solar project developers who cannot use unrated modules for debt financing. EcoVadis Platinum sustainability rating adds ESG-sensitive buyer appeal. VERIFIED
