Business Overview

Vikram Solar Limited (Kolkata, founded 2005; solar manufacturing since 2009) is one of India's largest solar photovoltaic module manufacturers, with roughly 9.5 GW of module capacity across its Falta SEZ (West Bengal) and Oragadam (Tamil Nadu) plants. V The company has cumulatively shipped over 10 GW of modules globally (25 million+ modules) and holds PVEL Top Performer recognition six years running, alongside BIS/IEC/UL certification and a NABL-accredited R&D lab. V It listed on the NSE/BSE on August 26, 2025 via a ₹2,079 Cr IPO (₹1,500 Cr fresh issue) priced at ₹332/share — the stock trades roughly 43% below that price today. V

The business is mid-pivot from a legacy EPC/module-supply mix (which produced losses through FY22 on fixed-price contract risk) toward pure-play module manufacturing with rapid backward integration: solar cells (targeting first cell output December 2026, PLI-backed 2.4 GW award), wafers/ingots (12 GW planned by FY30, ₹3,726 Cr Tamil Nadu Phase 1), and battery storage (15 GWh BESS by FY30 under the new "VION" brand). V

  • India's ALMM cell mandate (effective Jul 1, 2026) and PLI-II award structurally favour early integrated players

The case for caution

  • Promoter pledge fully released Feb-2026, then a fresh 5% pledge appeared in May-2026
  • Free cash flow remains thin (~₹26.5 Cr ttm) against a multi-year, multi-front capex program
  • Export revenue collapsed from 61.6% of sales (FY24) to ~1% (FY25) on US trade-policy shifts
