Economic Moat Assessment

PILLAR 1
Ontology / AI Operating System Switching Cost ★★★★★
The Ontology is a complete digital twin of an organization's operations — every data relationship, every workflow, every decision node mapped and integrated. Deploying it takes months and requires deep organizational knowledge sharing. Once deployed, replacing it would mean rebuilding this map from scratch — at enormous cost, operational risk, and downtime. For government clients (DoD, IRS), switching costs approach infinite: classified data can't easily migrate, and the cleared personnel relationship is irreplaceable. In commercial settings, supply chain and manufacturing clients have 100+ connected systems running through Foundry. The 139% NDR tells the story: clients don't leave. Moat Type: Switching Cost
Government Security Clearance Moat ★★★★★
Operating in classified environments with US government agencies requires facility clearances, personnel clearances, and system accreditations that take years to obtain. Palantir has had these for 20 years across the intelligence community, DoD, CIA, NSA, and allied governments. New entrants cannot replicate this in under 5–7 years regardless of technical capability. The $10B Army Enterprise Agreement signals that the DoD has made Palantir its default AI platform — a structural advantage no amount of VC funding can purchase. Moat Type: Regulatory / Intangible Asset
AIP Bootcamp Network Effect ★★★★
Palantir's 5-day "bootcamp" model lets enterprises deploy working AI prototypes in days rather than months. Each bootcamp generates productized templates — reusable workflow components that reduce deployment time for the next similar client. This creates an indirect network effect: more deployments → richer template library → faster proof of value → more deployments. The bootcamp pipeline has grown exponentially: US commercial TCV grew +342% YoY in Q3 2025. Moat Type: Network Effect (indirect) / Economies of Scale
20-Year AI Development Heritage ★★★★
Founded 2003. Built for intelligence agencies before deep learning was mainstream. The Gotham platform was handling unstructured data fusion from signals intelligence, human intelligence, and field reports before transformer models existed. This institutional knowledge and technology heritage cannot be replicated with money alone. When foundation models became commodity in 2023, Palantir was uniquely positioned to be the integration layer — exactly as Karp had predicted. Moat Type: Intangible Asset / Experience Curve
Pricing Power ★★★
Contracts are negotiated based on scope, data volume, users, and use cases — not list price. This creates pricing flexibility. The Army contract consolidation from 75 separate contracts to 1 Enterprise Agreement at $10B over 10 years suggests pricing power in the government segment. Commercial pricing is less transparent. NDR of 139% confirms upsell pricing power within existing accounts. Moat Type: Switching Cost + Pricing Power
🔄 Data Flywheel Assessment FORMING ★★★

PARTIAL YES. Palantir does not own clients' data. However, it accumulates something arguably more valuable: operational use-case knowledge — how a supply chain workflow maps to data, how fraud signals correlate with transactions, how battlefield command decisions flow through intelligence chains. Each deployment generates Ontology templates and workflow patterns that are proprietary to Palantir. No competitor has access to 20 years of classified government operational knowledge encoded into platform architectures. Confidence: ESTIMATED

Each new client deployment makes subsequent deployments faster and richer. Bootcamp conversion time is reportedly falling quarter over quarter. The marginal cost of each new Ontology deployment trends toward zero. This is a knowledge flywheel, not a raw ML data flywheel — but the compounding effect is real.
