Business Overview

GE Vernova T&D India Limited (NSE: GVT&D) is the India-listed electrification subsidiary of GE Vernova, the Cambridge, MA-based global energy company that spun out of General Electric in April 2024. The India entity traces its lineage to 1957 — through The English Electric Company, Alstom T&D, Areva T&D, and finally GE T&D. It was renamed to GE Vernova T&D India in October 2024.


The company builds power transmission and distribution infrastructure across the entire voltage range — from medium voltage up to ultra-high voltage (765 kV and beyond). Its product suite includes power transformers, gas-insulated switchgear (GIS), air-insulated switchgear (AIS), HVDC systems, FACTS, synchronous condensers, substation automation, and digital grid management software. It operates from five manufacturing facilities in India and has executed turnkey substation projects across India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh.

Why This Stock Is in the Series

India's energy transition is the single largest infrastructure investment theme of the next decade. The government's target of 500 GW of renewable capacity by 2030 requires an entirely rebuilt and expanded transmission grid — creating a 7-year secular capex cycle. GE Vernova T&D India is one of only two or three companies globally with the technology breadth to serve the full voltage spectrum of this build-out. The GridBeats AI/ML automation portfolio and EnergyAPM predictive analytics represent a genuine AI overlay on an already-strong infrastructure moat. The business quality is an A-grade story wearing a C-category badge. The valuation, however, is extreme — the stock has compounded 3,045% in five years, and the current 107x P/E leaves no margin of safety. This report establishes the entry framework so that when the inevitable correction arrives, we are ready to act decisively.
