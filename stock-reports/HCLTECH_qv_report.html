Business

HCLTech operates across three reporting lines: IT & Business Services (the core outsourced delivery engine — applications, infrastructure, digital, cloud), Engineering & R&D Services (ERS) — product engineering for automotive, telecom, semiconductors, medical devices, growing the fastest of the three, and HCLSoftware, a products business with $1.03bn ARR spanning data, security, automation and DevOps tooling. Leadership: C Vijayakumar (CEO & MD) and Roshni Nadar Malhotra (Chairperson). The company has paid a dividend for 90 consecutive quarters.

Why this series, why this category

HCLTech clears Category C — Mature Quality Compounder — cleanly on the numbers: ROIC has run 35–39% for several years straight, the balance sheet carries net cash of ~₹24,270 Cr (₹89.70/share), and FCF conversion has exceeded 120% of net income in the most recent fiscal year. That is "wide moat" territory by any standard scorecard. The growth profile (high-single to low-double-digit revenue CAGR, no longer 20%+ heady growth) and absence of a single, dominant non-discretionary monopoly position rule out Categories A and B; it is not commodity-priced or moat-less, which rules out E; and while it is investing aggressively in AI, its core product is not AI itself and FCF is solidly positive, which rules out F.

Why this stock is interesting right now

Two things are happening to HCLTech at the same time, and they pull in opposite directions. First, the stock has been structurally de-rated — down from a 52-week high of ₹1,780 to a recent low of ₹1,089, a roughly 34% drawdown, after Q4 FY26 results missed Street estimates on revenue, margin and deal-win growth, and FY27 guidance came in below expectations. Second, in the same week this report was built, HCLTech announced a $150 million lead investment in Sarvam AI (India's leading sovereign-AI startup) for a 10.5% stake — its most concrete bet yet that it can be an AI winner, not just an AI casualty. The framework's sector table tags IT Services as a structural 🔴 AI THREAT, and the evidence this session — analysts explicitly citing a 3–5% "AI deflation" drag on FY27 industry growth — supports keeping that flag. But HCLTech's response (OpenAI, Google Cloud, ServiceNow, Cisco partnerships; $620mn annualized "Advanced AI Revenue"; the Sarvam stake) is a genuinely credible pivot, not a marketing slide. That tension — clean compounder fundamentals colliding with a real, currently-unresolved AI-driven demand/margin question — is the whole report.
