Business Overview

The Revenue Illusion — Understanding Rajesh Exports

Rajesh Exports is a Bengaluru-based gold company founded in 1989 by brothers Rajesh and Prashant Mehta. It achieved genuine Fortune 500 status — ranked 7th on the Fortune India 500 in 2022 and 462nd on the Fortune Global 500 with revenues of ₹2.90 trillion. That ranking is the single most misleading data point about this business.

The core problem: Rajesh Exports' revenue is almost entirely the price of gold itself passing through its P&L. The company buys gold, refines it (at Valcambi) or manufactures it into jewellery (in India), and sells it at a thin spread. The "revenue" of ₹7.4 lakh crore reflects gold's value as a precious metal, not any economic value Rajesh Exports creates. When gold prices rise, revenues soar; when gold prices fall, revenues collapse — and profits throughout remain a rounding error of the top line.

The Valcambi Question — Why 35% of World's Gold Processing Is Not a Moat

The most compelling bull case for Rajesh Exports is: "They process 35% of world gold production. That scale must be worth something." Let's test this rigorously.

Why refinery scale ≠ Buffett moat: A gold refinery does not own the gold. It is paid a refining fee to convert raw/scrap gold into LBMA-certified bars. The gold goes in, the LBMA bar comes out, and the refiner keeps ~0.02-0.05% of the gold's value as a fee. This is a service business with near-zero pricing power because: (1) The service is standardised (LBMA certification is universal), (2) Multiple competing Swiss refiners (Metalor, PAMP, MKS, Argor-Heraeus) offer identical services, and (3) Banks and miners diversify refiner exposure for supply chain security.

The Valcambi conflict gold risk: Valcambi faces ongoing scrutiny for importing gold from conflict zones (Burkina Faso via Wafex/MM Multitrade, 2015 Berne Declaration report) and from Dubai-based Kaloti Group (accused of money laundering). While Valcambi contests these allegations and maintains LBMA compliance, the reputational overhang is real. Any regulatory action against Valcambi would devastate the entire Rajesh Exports investment case.
