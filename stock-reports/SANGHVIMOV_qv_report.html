What Sanghvi Movers Does

Established in 1989, Sanghvi Movers Limited (SML) is India's and Asia's largest crane rental company, ranked 5th globally by International Cranes. The company provides hydraulic truck-mounted telescopic and lattice boom cranes, and crawler cranes (20T–1,600T) to power, wind, infrastructure, refinery, steel, cement, and construction sectors across India and internationally (Vietnam, KSA, Botswana). SML operates from 17 strategically placed depots across India, giving it unmatched geographic reach for large-lift projects.

Three Business Segments

🏗
Crane Hiring & Ancillary Services
Core segment. India +35% in FY26. Fleet 370+ cranes. EBITDA margins 50–60% (standalone crane business).
💨
Wind EPC (Sangreen Future Renewables)
Asset-light. Logistics, intercarting, installation of wind turbines. High revenue, 10–12% EBITDA margins. Margin dilutive but recurring.
🌍
International Expansion (SMME - KSA)
Saudi Arabia subsidiary. High utilization. Vision 2030 TAM $800M–1B p.a. Pre-revenue ramp-up. Botswana operations commenced.

Why This Stock Is Interesting

SML sits at the intersection of three secular India tailwinds: the ₹111 lakh crore National Infrastructure Pipeline (NIP), India's 500 GW renewable energy ambition by 2030, and the industrial capex supercycle (steel, refinery, cement). As the dominant player with a replacement cost advantage (₹5,000+ Cr to replicate its fleet + depot network), SML captures outsized share of each upcycle. The Elevate 2030 strategy diversifies revenue away from pure cyclical crane rental into higher-frequency Wind EPC and international expansion — reducing earnings volatility at the cost of near-term margin compression.

Framework Adaptation: Sanghvi Movers is an asset-heavy industrial rental business. Maintenance CapEx split is critical — cranes depreciate at ~8–12% p.a. meaning D&A closely approximates maintenance CapEx. Owner Earnings ≈ Net Income + D&A − Full Replacement Allocation. FCF currently negative due to aggressive growth investment — this is intentional, not concerning, IF utilization and yields recover. Category C WACC 12–13%. Terminal P/E 18–22x.

Economic Moat Analysis

1. Efficient Scale — Fleet Replacement Cost Barrier
★★★★
To replicate SML's fleet of 370+ cranes (20T–1,600T), depot network, and operator pool would require ₹5,000+ Cr in capital investment and 5–7 years of ecosystem building. Heavy-lift crawlers (800T+) cost ₹15–30 Cr each and have 6–18 month delivery timelines. This is NOT a winner-takes-all moat (competitors like Crown Lifters are buying heavy cranes), but scale advantages are real. Stress test: HOLDS in downturns as competitors cannot easily add capacity.
2. Geographic Depot Network — Mobilization Advantage
★★★
17 strategically placed depots across India minimise mobilization and demobilization costs. For large infrastructure projects, crane availability speed matters as much as price. SML's network gives it a bid advantage of 5–10% on total project cost. Stress test: HOLDS — depot infrastructure is long-term, sticky.
3. Operator Expertise & Safety Track Record
★★★
India has severe shortage of certified crane operators. SML's 35+ year history means it has the deepest operator and rigger pool in India. Customers (L&T, NTPC, RIL, Adani) prefer SML for complex lifts precisely because of this track record. A failed lift (₹50 Cr+ equipment damage) is a risk companies avoid by choosing established operators. Stress test: STRENGTHENS — operators become more scarce, not less, in a growth cycle.
4. Customer Relationships & Long-term Contracts
★★★
Multi-year agreements with India's largest infrastructure companies (L&T Hydrocarbon, NTPC, Adani, RIL Jamnagar, Deepak Fertilisers). Contract switching costs include: requalification process, mobilization costs, operator familiarity with plant. Stress test: HOLDS — contracts are typically 3–5 years; provides earnings floor in downturns.
5. Global Positioning (5th Largest Globally)
★★
International ranking opens doors to multinational project developers (especially in KSA, Africa) who require global-standard operators. SMME (Saudi Arabia) leveraging Vision 2030 pipeline. Stress test: WEAKENS in conflict/geopolitical scenarios — KSA Strait of Hormuz risk is real.

Management's thesis: India's infrastructure supercycle window is 5–8 years, and fleet availability is the binding constraint on revenue growth. This makes sense IF utilization and yields recover. The risk is over-ordering cranes in a cyclical business.
