Business Overview

Cummins India Limited (CIL), founded in 1962 and headquartered in Pune, is the oldest Cummins entity in India. It designs, manufactures, distributes and services diesel and natural-gas engines (2.8–100 litre displacement) and power generator sets (up to 3,000 kW / 3,750 kVA). VERIFIED The company operates through three business units: Engine Business (transport, industrial, off-highway), Power Systems Business (data centres, hospitals, telecom, utilities), and Distribution Business (aftermarket parts, service, rental).

MNC Subsidiary Context

Cummins India is 51% owned by Cummins Inc. (NYSE: CMI), a $38B revenue, century-old industrial giant headquartered in Columbus, Indiana. CMI itself has been re-rated as an AI data-centre infrastructure play (shares up ~50% in 6 months as of March 2026). Key implications for CIL: (1) Technology access guaranteed from parent — emission-compliant engines, advanced gensets. (2) Limited strategic autonomy — CIL's product roadmap, exports, and pricing are partly governed by CMI's global strategy. (3) Transfer pricing risk — ~34% of CIL revenues are exports (back to CMI group entities), making terms an ongoing management integrity focus. (4) Dividend is high (~71% payout) partly because CMI wants cash returns — minority shareholders benefit but retained-capital reinvestment is constrained.

MNC Subsidiary Governance Discount

Minority shareholders in MNC subsidiaries face an inherent structural issue: the parent (Cummins Inc.) controls strategic direction, product mix, export volumes, and inter-company pricing. This creates three risks: (1) Product withdrawal risk — CMI may shift production to other subsidiaries globally if strategic priorities change. (2) Transfer pricing risk — arms-length pricing on exports to group entities is audited but not independently verifiable. (3) Delisting risk — historically some MNCs have delisted Indian subsidiaries once they become too valuable; at ₹1.33L Cr market cap, this is less likely but remains a tail risk. Assessment: These are known, manageable risks. CIL has been publicly listed for 32 years with clean governance. Management grade does not fall below B+ on account of these structural features.
