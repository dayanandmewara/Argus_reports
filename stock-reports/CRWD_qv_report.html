Platform Architecture

The Falcon Platform — One Agent, Infinite Modules

Falcon is a single lightweight agent deployed across millions of endpoints. Unlike legacy multi-agent architectures, one Falcon sensor feeds all security functions — endpoint (EDR), cloud workloads (CNAPP), identity (ITP), SIEM (Next-Gen), and browser protection — into a single data lake called the Threat Graph. This architectural choice, made at founding in 2011, turns out to be the defining competitive moat of the AI era: unified telemetry creates a training data advantage that cannot be replicated by assembling multiple point products.

As of FY2026: 30+ Falcon modules available. Module adoption continuing to expand: 50% of customers use 6+ modules, 34% use 7+, 24% use 8+. Falcon Flex (platform subscription model) ARR reached $1.69B (+120% YoY), representing 32% of total ARR. FY2027 guidance projects ending ARR of $6.47-6.52B (+24%) with $5.87-5.93B in revenue.

Special Context: July 2024 Outage & Recovery

On July 19, 2024, a flawed Falcon sensor content update crashed 8.5 million Windows systems worldwide — the largest IT outage in history. The incident triggered securities class actions, significant reputational damage, and fears of permanent customer attrition. CrowdStrike's response: immediate technical remediation, CEO George Kurtz on all major networks, and the "Customer Commitment Package" (Falcon Flex credits). The result: >95% enterprise customer retention, record Q3 FY2026 net new ARR of $265M (+73% YoY), and $5.25B ending ARR in FY2026. The outage ultimately validated the switching cost moat — customers had no viable alternative at scale. This is now a closed risk, not an ongoing one.
