WHAT TSMC DOES

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC), founded in 1987 by Morris Chang, is the world's largest dedicated semiconductor foundry — a company that manufactures chips for other companies' designs (fabless model). TSMC does not design chips itself; it builds them for Apple, NVIDIA, AMD, Qualcomm, Broadcom, and virtually every other advanced semiconductor company on Earth. Its business is essentially a monopoly in advanced chip fabrication at 3nm and below.

The company operates at the absolute frontier of human engineering. Its 3nm process (N3) is the workhorse for today's AI accelerators; its 2nm (N2) — using Gate-All-Around transistor architecture — entered volume production in late 2025 and scales through 2026. No other company can manufacture these chips at commercial scale with comparable yields. Samsung has tried. Intel is trying. Neither is competitive at the leading edge today.

WHY TSMC IS IN THE SERIES

TSMC represents the rarest class of investment: a structural monopoly at the chokepoint of an existential technology buildout. The global AI infrastructure requires chips that only TSMC can manufacture at commercial scale. This positions it as the picks-and-shovels beneficiary of every AI model, every hyperscaler data centre, every autonomous vehicle compute stack.

The Buffett framework applies powerfully here: TSMC has a wide economic moat (process technology + customer lock-in), a financial fortress (ROE 35%+, net margins 40-50%), exceptional management (C.C. Wei's 40-year semiconductor career), and is now emerging from a period of intensive reinvestment — the classic setup for FCF inflection.

⚠ PRIMARY RISK: TAIWAN STRAIT GEOPOLITICS

This is not a company to dismiss or understate the risk. Bloomberg Economics estimates a Taiwan conflict would cost ~$10 trillion globally (~10% of world GDP). Prediction markets imply ~22% conflict probability by 2030. TSMC's mitigation: Arizona fabs (2nm volume production by 2028-2030), Japan (Kumamoto operational), $100B+ US investment commitment. Most leading-edge capacity remains in Taiwan through 2028.

This risk demands a wider margin of safety and position sizing discipline.

MOAT SOURCES — DETAILED ASSESSMENT
① PROCESS TECHNOLOGY MONOPOLY ★★★★★
TSMC is the only company in the world that can manufacture chips at 3nm and 2nm at commercial scale with competitive yields. Samsung has 3nm GAA but yield issues. Intel is attempting 18A but not competitive yet. This advantage compounds: each new node requires $5-10B in additional R&D and years of learning curves. TSMC's 35-year head start cannot be closed by capital alone — it requires institutional knowledge that takes decades to build.
STRENGTHENS in AI cycle
② CUSTOMER LOCK-IN / SWITCHING COSTS ★★★★★
Apple's M4, NVIDIA's Blackwell, AMD's EPYC — these chips are co-designed with TSMC's process technology. Switching foundries requires redesigning the entire chip from scratch (18-24 months + $1B+) and accepting lower performance and yields. Customers like Apple (25% of TSMC revenue) are structurally committed for 5-10 year horizons. TSMC's process node advantages make switching economically irrational for leading-edge customers.
HOLDS / strengthens
③ SCALE ECONOMICS & COST ADVANTAGES ★★★★★
TSMC spreads its R&D and equipment costs across a revenue base 3× larger than Samsung Foundry. This creates an inescapable cost advantage: the more TSMC produces, the cheaper its per-wafer cost, the lower its price, the more customers come. This virtuous cycle makes it progressively harder for challengers to match TSMC's economics even if they close the technology gap.
STRENGTHENS
④ ADVANCED PACKAGING MONOPOLY (CoWoS) ★★★★☆
TSMC's CoWoS (Chip on Wafer on Substrate) advanced packaging is sold out through 2026 with demand growing 113% annually (per industry reports). Every major AI accelerator — NVIDIA Blackwell, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium — requires this technology to stack chips and high-bandwidth memory. CoWoS capacity is the single biggest bottleneck for AI chip production globally. This is an emerging monopoly within TSMC's broader business.
FORMING — AI-CRITICAL
⑤ TALENT & INSTITUTIONAL KNOWLEDGE ★★★★☆
83,000+ employees with deep semiconductor manufacturing expertise. Average tenure is 10+ years. TSMC operates in Hsinchu Science Park (Taiwan's semiconductor cluster) where competing for this talent from a greenfield position is virtually impossible. Arizona fab's reported challenge: US talent with comparable process expertise is scarce, explaining why TSMC brought Taiwanese engineers to train Arizona workers.

SECTION E — AI DISRUPTION RESISTANCE ASSESSMENT
MOAT RESILIENCE SCORE: 9.5 / 10 — VIRTUALLY AI-DISRUPTION-PROOF

There is no AI pathway to disrupting TSMC's core business. You cannot use AI to manufacture a semiconductor. The physics of photolithography, etching, and atomic-scale deposition cannot be replaced by software. AI can HELP with design (chip design AI is real), but the physical manufacturing step — which is TSMC's business — cannot be virtualised or automated away.

The only scenario where TSMC faces meaningful disruption: a fundamentally different computing paradigm (quantum, neuromorphic, optical) that does not require silicon chips. Timeline: 15-25 years if ever. Not an investable risk within a 5-10 year horizon.
