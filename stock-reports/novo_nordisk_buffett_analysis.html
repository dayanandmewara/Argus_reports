Company Overview

Business Model

Novo Nordisk, founded in 1923 in Denmark, is the world's leading company in diabetes and obesity care. Its core business rests on two revolutionary drug classes: GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide — branded as Ozempic for T2D and Wegovy for obesity) and insulin products. A third segment — Rare Disease (haemophilia, rare endocrine disorders) — contributes ~10% of revenue. The company pioneered semaglutide, a modified GLP-1 hormone that suppresses appetite, controls blood sugar, and has demonstrated cardiovascular mortality benefits. Ozempic and Wegovy together generated ~$32B in FY2025 revenue, representing 67% of total sales. VERIFIED

Why This Stock Is In The Series

NVO is the quintessential Category C compounder encountering a rare, potentially temporary ROIC inflection point. At $36 — a P/E of ~10x on normalised earnings — the market is treating a trough year as a structural impairment. Buffett's framework is built for exactly this moment: separating permanent moat erosion from cyclical pricing and pipeline disappointment. This is the most interesting risk/reward entry in global pharma in 2026, and testing the AI WIDENER framework in the drug discovery context is uniquely valuable to the series.

Overall Moat Assessment

Moat Grade: A− (Wide, with Competitive Narrowing in Progress)

The A− grade reflects: (1) an indisputably exceptional historical moat built on biological IP, 100-year data, manufacturing scale, and clinical evidence; (2) real and ongoing narrowing pressure from Eli Lilly's clinical efficacy advantage (tirzepatide > semaglutide at current doses); (3) pricing power erosion from US government drug pricing reforms.
