Business Overview
Fortinet (NASDAQ: FTNT) is the global leader in the convergence of networking and security, providing its integrated Security Fabric platform to enterprises, service providers, governments, and SMBs across 170+ countries. Founded in 2000 by brothers Ken Xie (CEO) and Michael Xie (CTO), the company invented the world's first purpose-built network security ASIC, enabling performance-to-cost advantages unmatched by software-only peers. FY2025 revenue of $6.80B (+14%) is underpinned by a $6.36B deferred revenue backlog, giving high revenue visibility. The business is 67% services (recurring subscriptions and support), with a Rule of 40 score of ~47, confirming quality software economics.

Why This Stock Is in the Series

Fortinet is the most profitable large-cap pure-play cybersecurity vendor in the world. Its unique combination of proprietary FortiASIC chips, unified FortiOS operating system, and the Security Fabric's breadth creates structural cost advantages over software-only peers. The cyber threat landscape is structurally worsening (AI-powered attacks, 85% of professionals cite GenAI as a threat driver per Fortinet's own 2025 report), creating a secular tailwind independent of the economic cycle. The forming FortiAI data flywheel — 5.6M sensors generating real-time threat telemetry — is the series' cleanest example of a Legacy Incumbent becoming an AI WIDENER. With $6.36B in deferred revenue and $2.21B FCF, the financial fortress is exceptional. The challenge: the stock has de-rated from its $114 ATH and remains above the 30% MoS entry zone, making patience the primary discipline required.
