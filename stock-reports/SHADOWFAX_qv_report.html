Business Profile

Shadowfax Technologies Limited is a technology-led third-party logistics (3PL) company that enables digital commerce across India. Founded in 2015 by four IIT Delhi alumni — Abhishek Bansal (CEO), Vaibhav Khandelwal (CTO), Praharsh Chandra, and Gaurav Jaithliya — the company has grown from a hyperlocal courier into India's largest 3PL provider by order volume in same-day delivery, reverse logistics, and quick commerce. VERIFIED

The business operates through three revenue segments: (1) Express Services — forward deliveries, reverse pickups, hand-in-hand exchanges, prime deliveries; (2) Hyperlocal Services — quick commerce, food delivery (Swiggy, Zomato, Blinkit, Zepto, BigBasket, Uber); and (3) Other Logistics — critical shipments, dark store operations, unbundled services.

🔄 Data Flywheel Assessment
Q1: Proprietary Training Data?
SF Maps is trained on 1.5 billion+ delivery and pickup data points accumulated over Shadowfax's operating history across 14,758 pin codes. India's addressing complexity (incomplete addresses, landmarks, dialect variations) means this data is uniquely valuable and non-replicable by new entrants. VERIFIED — CTO statement and IPO DRHP.
Q2: Does AI Improve with More Data/Users?
SF Maps uses an ANN model with Siamese Network architecture. As more deliveries are completed, more address-resolution outcomes are fed back into the model, improving prediction accuracy. Current accuracy: >90% within 100 metres. Cancellations reduced by 10%. Mechanism is confirmed: more deliveries → better address resolution → fewer failed deliveries → cost reduction. VERIFIED — CTO statement July 2024.
Q3: Does Better AI Attract More Users?
Better delivery accuracy → lower RTO (Return-to-Origin) rates → lower cost for e-commerce clients → Shadowfax wins more enterprise contracts. The loop is plausible but not yet confirmed with quantitative data on client acquisition driven specifically by SF Maps accuracy. Loop speed: quarterly (contract renewal cycles). ESTIMATED

Section B — Data Flywheel Deep-Dive

🔄 SF Maps: The India Addressing AI
Q1: What is the proprietary training data?
1.5+ billion delivery and pickup data points accumulated since 2015. This includes: GPS coordinates of actual delivery completions, address text vs. actual location mapping, failed delivery cause codes, landmark-based navigation outcomes, and multi-language address variants across 14,758 pin codes. Uniqueness: No competitor has equivalent delivery-outcome data at this scale for India's chaotic addressing environment. Google Maps has routing data but NOT delivery completion/failure outcomes which are the gold standard training labels. VERIFIED — CTO statement, DRHP.
Q2: How does AI improve with more data?
Each delivery adds an outcome label: did this address interpretation lead to successful delivery? SF Maps' ANN model (Siamese Network architecture) learns address resolution accuracy from these outcomes. Evidence: >90% accuracy within 100 metres achieved, delivery cancellations reduced 10%. As Shadowfax scales from 44 Cr orders (FY25) to an estimated 63 Cr orders (FY26), the model receives ~19 Cr new training examples per year. Measurable improvement confirmed. VERIFIED from company press release, July 2024.
Q3: Does better AI attract more users?
Better address resolution → lower RTO (Return to Origin) rates → e-commerce clients save 3–5% of revenue on failed deliveries → Shadowfax wins/retains contracts. The RTO Predictor (Shadowfax 360) adds a second loop: AI predicts before shipment which orders will be returned, allowing clients to take preventive action. This creates a direct ROI narrative for client acquisition. Loop plausible and commercially logical, but specific client acquisition data attributable to SF Maps not yet disclosed. ESTIMATED
