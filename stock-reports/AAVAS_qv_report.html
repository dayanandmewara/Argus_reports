Company Overview

Business Description

Aavas Financiers is a retail affordable housing finance company (HFC) founded in 2011 in Jaipur, focused on home loans, loans against property (LAP), and MSME loans to low- and middle-income self-employed customers in semi-urban and rural India. Its distinguishing edge: proprietary in-house underwriting for customers without formal income proof (ITR), using field-based assessment and decades of granular data on informal-sector borrowers.

With 435 branches across 15 states and ₹234.5 bn AUM as of FY26, Aavas addresses a structurally under-financed segment where traditional banks are structurally absent. Average ticket size is ₹0.98 mn — firmly affordable housing territory. The company operates 100% through an in-house loan origination model (no DSA), ensuring superior credit culture and data ownership.

Category & Framework Rationale

Category C — Mature Quality Compounder: ROIC 15–40% range, durable niche moat, stable AUM CAGR 15–20%. Housing finance framework adaptation applies: P/B Gordon Growth Model is primary valuation; NIM, GNPA, and Justified P/B replace standard ROIC metrics. No DCF — instead Excess Return Model.

AI Overlay ⚪ NEUTRAL: Aavas uses technology/AI for process efficiency (TAT reduction 13→6 days, 59% paper cut, digital agreements, AI-driven credit checks) but has not yet demonstrated a proprietary data flywheel that measurably attracts customers via AI superiority. FORMING status — potential to upgrade to WIDENER if rural credit AI becomes a structural moat.

The stock declined ~47% from its Dec 2025 peak of ₹2,234 to a March 2026 low of ₹1,060 — driven by leadership transition, AUM growth guidance cut, and HFC sector headwinds. Q4FY26 delivered a decisive operational rebound: record disbursements (₹23.5bn, +36% QoQ), NIMs expanding 44bps to 8.45%, and GNPA improving to 1.05%. Economic Moat Analysis

Proprietary Underwriting — Core Moat

★★★★

Aavas's defining edge is the ability to assess creditworthiness of self-employed informal-income borrowers without ITR/formal documents — using field-based income verification, asset assessment, cash flow proxies (utility bills, local business reputation), and 13+ years of outcome data. Banks structurally cannot do this at scale. This creates a captive market of ~60-70% of India's workforce.

Macro stress test: STRENGTHENS — in a rural income stress scenario, formal-income borrowers become more scarce; Aavas's non-formal underwriting skill becomes a larger moat.

Geographic Lock-In / Distribution

★★★

Contiguous expansion model — 435 branches across 15 states, deep local knowledge. In-house sourcing (no DSA) means Aavas owns the customer relationship end-to-end. First-mover advantage in semi-urban Rajasthan, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh — now extending to Tamil Nadu, South India.

Macro stress test: HOLDS — geographic diversification protects from regional stress (as seen with Maharashtra/MP stress contained).

Asset Quality / Credit Culture

★★★★

GNPA of 1.05% is among the lowest in the entire NBFC-HFC universe despite serving high-risk customers. This reflects institutional credit culture, not just benign macro — demonstrated through multiple cycles. Credit cost guided sustainably below 25bps. The ability to lend to risky segments with low NPAs is a genuine competitive advantage.

Macro stress test: HOLDS — even in stress, secured nature (avg LTV ~55%) limits loss given default materially.

Cost of Funds Advantage

★★

AAA-rated borrowings from DFIs (IFC, ADB, CDC/BII) at competitive rates — 83% of DCM from development finance institutions. Well-diversified liability franchise with 35+ lenders limits concentration risk. However, this advantage narrows as balance sheet scales and NHB refinancing share reduces.

Macro stress test: WEAKENS slightly — rate hike cycles compress NIMs as new borrowings price higher; witnessed FY22-FY25.

✓ CONFIRMED
