Economic Moat Assessment
Regional Brand & Reputation
★★★★★

Recognisable brand in NCR for trauma and super-specialty care. Healthcare brand loyalty is high — patients return to familiar institutions. Brand moat is regional (NCR/UP), not national, limiting pricing power vs Apollo/Max.

HOLDS in stress — healthcare brand is counter-cyclical.
Regulatory / Licensing Barriers
★★★★★

NABH accreditation, fire safety clearances, medical licenses, and regulatory approvals create meaningful entry barriers. A new hospital takes 3–5 years to become operational. Time moat for incumbents.

STRENGTHENS in stress — regulatory burden deters new entrants.
Specialist Doctor Network
★★★★★

Yatharth hires "star doctors" in oncology, neurosciences, nephrology. Patient follow-through is doctor-centric in India. Double-edged: strong when doctors stay, fragile when poached.

WEAKENS in stress — star doctors can be poached by larger chains.
Geographic Catchment
★★★★★

7 hospitals across NCR/UP provides geographic concentration (strength) and single-market risk (weakness). Scale is sub-critical vs Apollo/Max — fixed-cost leverage still developing.

Patient Switching Costs
★★★★★

Moderate switching costs — oncology patients, transplant follow-ups rarely switch mid-treatment. TPA/insurance empanellment creates lock-in. Stronger in chronic/complex care than OPD.
