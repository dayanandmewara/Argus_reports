Business Overview

HDFC Bank Limited, incorporated in 1994 and headquartered in Mumbai, is India's largest private sector bank by assets and market capitalisation. It operates across three primary verticals: Retail Banking (individuals, salaried professionals, MSMEs), Wholesale Banking (corporates, FIs, government entities), and Treasury (forex, derivatives, sovereign securities). With 9,689 branches and 21,176 ATMs across 4,170 cities as of March 2026, HDFC Bank is the quintessential deposit franchise — a 30-year institution woven into India's financial fabric.


The landmark event in HDFC Bank's modern history was the merger of Housing Development Finance Corporation Limited (HDFC Ltd) into HDFC Bank effective July 1, 2023. This created a combined entity with assets exceeding ₹44 lakh Crore — making it one of the world's top 10 banks by market capitalisation. However, the merger also introduced two transitional headwinds that have defined FY24-FY26: (1) NIM compression as HDFC Ltd's higher-cost funding mix and lower-yielding home loans diluted the bank's legacy NIM from ~4.3% to ~3.38%, and (2) elevated credit-to-deposit (CD) ratio requiring deliberate deposit mobilisation above loan growth.


The investment thesis for April 2026 is fundamentally a quality compounder at a cyclically depressed valuation with a meaningful earnings recovery path. ROA has begun recovering (1.94% FY26 vs 1.91% FY25), GNPA improved to 1.15% — best-in-class, credit costs fell to 35 bps from 48 bps. The stock trades at 2.28x P/B vs a historical 3–4x range, implying a built-in re-rating opportunity of 30–60% as NIM normalises to 3.6–3.8% and ROE recovers toward 16–17%.

Shareholding as Moat Amplifier

HDFC Bank has zero promoter holding post-merger (ex-HDFC Ltd was the promoter; now merged into the bank). This makes HDFC Bank unique — it is effectively a widely-held institution with 47.67% FII + 26.66% Mutual Fund + 7.18% Insurance = ~81.5% institutional ownership. Free float is essentially 100%.


This structure means: (1) No promoter pledge risk — one of the cleanest shareholding structures in Indian large-cap; (2) FII ownership at ~47.67% approaching sectoral FII investment cap (74% for private banks) → still 26% headroom; (3) Global sovereign wealth funds and long-duration institutional holders → quality of ownership is exceptional; (4) Any FII cap exhaustion fears (historically a HDFC Bank issue) are well managed with current utilisation at ~64%.

AI Disruption Assessment

MOAT RESILIENCE SCORE: 9/10 — HDFC Bank's core moats (deposit franchise, switching costs, D-SIB status, brand) are among the most AI-resistant in India. Reasons:
