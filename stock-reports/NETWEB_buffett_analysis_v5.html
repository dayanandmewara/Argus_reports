Business Model — India's AI Server Integrator

Core business (Tyrone brand): Netweb designs, manufactures, and deploys high-end computing systems under its proprietary "Tyrone" brand. Products: (1) AI Systems & Enterprise Workstations (64.2% of Q3 FY26 revenue — fastest growing), (2) HPC Supercomputing, (3) Private Cloud / Hyperconverged Infrastructure, (4) High-Performance Storage, (5) Data Centre Servers, (6) Network Switches (nascent).

Make in India manufacturing advantage: Netweb operates a manufacturing facility in Faridabad, Haryana + commissioned a new high-end computing server/storage/switch facility in May 2024. This "Make in India" positioning is critical for: (1) Government/defence contracts requiring indigenous manufacture, (2) PLI scheme benefits, (3) Faster delivery vs 6–12 month import lead times, (4) Post-sales service and maintenance.

NVIDIA partnership (Tyrone AI Systems): Netweb is an authorised NVIDIA MGX partner, integrating GB200 NVL4, H100, and other NVIDIA GPU platforms into full-stack AI systems. The Q3 FY26 "strategic order of national significance" (₹450 Cr) involved deploying NVIDIA-powered AI compute infrastructure for a government/national AI initiative.

Revenue model: Project-based (not SaaS/recurring). Significant seasonality — Q4 and Q3 are peak quarters; Q1 always sees sharp QoQ decline. Revenue is lumpy: single large orders can double a quarter (as happened in Q3 FY26). This lumpiness is a defining characteristic — investors must evaluate annual performance, not quarterly.

vs SMCI (direct comparison): Netweb is India's Supermicro analogue — but with important differences: (1) No fraud or integrity concerns, (2) Government/defence moat from Make in India, (3) Much smaller scale (₹1,149 Cr vs SMCI $22B revenue), (4) Higher gross margins (21% vs SMCI's 6–13%), (5) Only one market (India) vs SMCI's global reach. Netweb is the earlier-stage, higher-integrity version of a similar business model.

The Central Investment Tension: Netweb sits at the intersection of India's most powerful structural tailwind (AI infrastructure buildout) and the most demanding valuation requirements. The business is real, growing fast, and uniquely positioned. But at ₹3,282 (P/E ~105x TTM), the stock prices in many years of perfect execution. Simultaneously, two rounds of promoter selling in 3 months raise governance questions that the framework cannot ignore. The verdict is WATCH — the price and promoter signals prevent a BUY, but the business quality prevents a PASS.

Make in India Manufacturing Monopoly
★★★★½

The most durable moat: government procurement regulations increasingly mandate domestic manufacturing for strategic AI compute. Netweb is the ONLY large-scale Make in India HPC/AI server manufacturer in India. Foreign players (Dell, HPE, Supermicro) cannot access sovereign AI tenders. This regulatory moat is widening, not narrowing, as India's AI self-reliance strategy intensifies.

NVIDIA MGX Partner — India Exclusivity
★★★★

Netweb is one of India's first NVIDIA MGX-certified partners for Blackwell (GB200 NVL4) integration. NVIDIA MGX certification is not freely available — it requires engineering validation, manufacturing compliance, and NVIDIA's explicit approval. Netweb's GB200 liquid-cooled systems are a rare capability in India, giving it first-mover advantage in the highest-value AI compute segment.

25-Year Institutional Relationships
★★★½

Founded 1999. Relationships with ISRO, IITs, IIMs, national data centres, and government AI agencies span 25+ years. In B2G (business-to-government) markets, vendor trust and relationship depth are as important as technical specifications. Netweb's institutional memory is a genuine switching cost — agencies who deployed Netweb systems for HPC 10 years ago naturally turn to Netweb for AI upgrades.

Liquid Cooling Technical Expertise
★★★

High-density GPU clusters require sophisticated thermal management. Netweb's DLC (Direct Liquid Cooling) capability, validated with NVIDIA and Vertiv partnerships, is technically differentiated vs basic server assemblers. Not unique globally (SMCI, Asus have similar), but in India, this is a niche capability that takes years to develop.

Skylus.ai — AI Management Platform
★★★

Proprietary AI resource management platform launched FY25. If Skylus.ai gains traction as the management layer for India's AI compute installations, it creates a software-driven moat layer ON TOP of the hardware moat — potentially building the data flywheel described above. Watch this carefully — it is the option value that could reprice Netweb from Cat B to Cat F characteristics over time.
