Business Overview

Aye Finance Limited (NSE: AYE) is a Delhi/Gurugram-based NBFC–Middle Layer (NBFC-ML) focused exclusively on the massively underpenetrated micro-MSME credit market. Founded in 2014 by Sanjay Sharma (MD, ex-Citi/ICICI/Fullerton) and Vikram Jetley, the company provides small-ticket business loans averaging ₹1.8 lakh per disbursement to micro-enterprises in manufacturing, trading, services, and agri-allied sectors.

The company's distinctive "cluster-based underwriting" methodology analyses 70+ business clusters (e.g., hosiery makers in Ludhiana, leather workers in Agra) using both financial and non-financial inputs, augmented by data science and AI models. This allows lending to enterprises with limited formal documentation — businesses banks routinely reject. As of FY26, it manages a ₹7,044 Cr AUM across 568 branches in 18 states and 3 UTs, serving 6.4 lakh+ customers since inception.

The company listed on NSE/BSE on February 16, 2026 at ₹129 per share, raising ₹710 Cr in fresh equity. Backed by marquee investors including Elevation Capital, CapitalG (Google's growth fund), and British International Investment, the company has attracted institutional conviction at its core.

Why This Stock Is In The Series

IPO Base Breakout Thesis: The stock dipped from its IPO price of ₹129 to a low of ₹93 before forming a classic 10-week consolidation base. On April 27, 2026, Q4 FY26 results triggered a +12.8% surge with a new 52-week high of ₹158. This is exactly the Phase 2→3 institutional re-entry signal the framework identifies for AI-enabled NBFCs in the credit scoring AI adoption curve.

Structural Opportunity: India's formal credit gap for micro-enterprises is estimated at ₹32-40 trillion. Aye Finance's current AUM of ₹7,044 Cr represents a fraction of this TAM. With AI credit scoring expanding the serviceable market to unscored borrowers, the growth runway is among the longest in Indian financial services.

Credit Cycle Inflection: GNPA peaked at 4.94% in Q3 FY26 and has begun declining (4.80% in Q4 FY26). Credit costs have fallen for five consecutive quarters — a classic cyclical inflection signal in NBFC analysis.

Framework Adaptation Needed: As an NBFC, negative operating cash flows are the norm (disbursements are classified as OCF). Owner Earnings methodology is replaced by deposit-franchise/NIM-based analysis. DCF uses P/BV justified via Gordon Growth. Earnings quality test adjusts for NBFC norms.

ROIC / ROA as Moat Proof
For NBFCs, ROA is the primary capital efficiency metric (not ROIC). ROA of 4.6% in Q4 FY26 is world-class for micro-MSME lending, approaching the 5%+ tier of India's best NBFCs. This confirms the moat is real: high margins on a high-risk portfolio means the underwriting model is genuinely superior.

ROIC is not directly comparable for leveraged financial businesses. The proxy — Return on Equity of 16% (Q4 FY26) on an unleveraged post-IPO equity base — confirms the business can generate mid-to-high-teen returns even with conservative provisioning.

Section B — Data Flywheel Deep-Dive
Q1: What is Aye's Proprietary Training Data?
Data Asset: 12+ years of repayment behavior across 70+ micro-enterprise business clusters. Includes: (a) cash-flow inference from physical business assessment, (b) outcome labels — who defaulted and when (critical for supervised learning), (c) cluster-level market cycle patterns (e.g., how hosiery industry in Ludhiana behaves through cotton price cycles), (d) 45.4% repeat borrower cohort with full credit history.

VERIFIED from RHP + earnings calls

Uniqueness Assessment: No other NBFC has equivalent cluster-level micro-data in India. Banks don't lend to this segment systematically. Other micro-NBFCs have narrower geographic reach. Data quality score: 2/3
Q2: How Does AI Improve with More Data?
Mechanism: Each new borrower in a cluster adds to the outcome dataset. The AI model learns which cluster-specific signals (type of equipment, supplier relationships, seasonal patterns) best predict repayment. Management states the data science models are used for underwriting enhancement across all 70+ clusters.

Evidence: Repeat borrower rate improved from 25.7% (FY23) to 45.4% (FY25) — suggesting AI models are better identifying good borrowers within clusters. GNPA falling from 4.94% (Q3) to 4.80% (Q4) despite 27% AUM growth indicates the models are improving risk discrimination.

ESTIMATED — No specific model accuracy metric disclosed in earnings calls. Score: 2.5/4
Q3: Does Better AI Attract More Users?
Loop: Better AI → fewer defaults in cluster → lower EMI pricing possible → more borrowers attracted within cluster → more data → better AI. The loop exists but operates on a 12-18 month cycle (loan tenure), making it slower than digital platform flywheels.

Evidence: 70,841 new borrowers in Q4 FY26 alone. Collection efficiency of 99.5% in March 2026 — word-of-mouth credibility in clusters that this lender "works." However, borrowers don't choose Aye for its AI — they choose it because it's the only formal credit option. The AI reduces cost and improves approval rates, indirectly attracting more applicants.

Score: 1.5/3 — Loop confirmed but slow-cycling
