0Overview

Category + rationale · business snapshot · why this stock is interesting right now

Why Category D, not C or B

Suzlon spent 2015–2023 in and out of financial distress — a CDR debt restructuring, promoter pledges as high as 36%, and years of losses or wafer-thin margins. A 2023 rights issue cleared the debt; FY26 closed with ₹2,384 Cr net cash and a record order book. That's the textbook Category D setup: "below-threshold metrics improving, event-driven thesis." It is not yet Category C (mature quality compounder) because the clean track record is short — ROE was negative in FY22 and a distorted 263% in FY23 on one-off items, so the 5-year consistency bar Category C requires isn't met yet. It's not Category A or B either: this is industrial capital goods, not a consumer monopoly or a tech-style compounder.

Business snapshot

Suzlon designs, manufactures, installs and services wind turbine generators (WTGs) — blades, towers, gearboxes, nacelles, largely in-house. It is India's largest wind energy company: ~21 GW installed across 17 countries, and an operations & maintenance (O&M) business servicing over 14,000 turbines. Its S144 platform (3 MW class, built for India's lower wind-speed sites and narrow rural roads) is the dominant product in the domestic market, with ~9 GW of cumulative order intake.

What makes this one interesting — and hard

Interesting: a genuine, largely-completed balance-sheet turnaround sitting inside a structural, government-backed sector tailwind (India's wind capacity target jumps from 56 GW to 100 GW by 2030). New CEO Ajay Kapur and a "Suzlon 2.0" strategy target 25%+ revenue CAGR through FY31 by moving from turbine-only OEM to integrated EPC, hybrid, storage and asset-management player.

Hard: the stock is up ~50% off its March 2026 low ahead of this report, reported FY26 profit leaned on a ₹742 Cr non-cash deferred-tax-asset recognition, and management itself flagged muted quarterly order inflow and a slower-than-hoped EPC transition on the Q4 call. Q1 FY27 results land July 9 — four days from this report — as the next real data point.

Solar+BESS hybrid economics are also starting to compete for the same tender rupee as pure wind.

4Management

Strengths · concerns · pledges · capital allocation

Strengths

Successfully executed a multi-year balance-sheet turnaround: from CDR restructuring and heavy pledging in the 2010s to net-cash by FY26. Leadership refresh in 2026 — Ajay Kapur as Group CEO, J P Chalasani elevated — paired with a specific, numbered strategy ("Suzlon 2.0": 10 GW annual RE sales, 70 GW AUM, 25%+ revenue CAGR to FY31). That's a genuinely more measurable set of commitments than most turnaround stories offer.

Concerns

SEBI penalised Suzlon ₹15.95 Cr in May 2026 over misstatements in FY2013-14 to FY2017-18 financial statements, overturning a 2025 exoneration; the company is appealing to the Securities Appellate Tribunal and states no impact on current operations. It's a legacy issue from the distress-era management, not the current team, but it's a live legal matter worth tracking. Separately, promoter holding (11.7%) is low by Indian standards — though this reflects the dilution needed to fix the balance sheet, not an active promoter sell-down, since the stake has been flat for at least two quarters.
