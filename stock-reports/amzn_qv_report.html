Classification & Business Overview
The Amazon Empire — Three Interlocking Flywheels

Amazon.com is the world's largest e-commerce platform, the dominant cloud computing provider, and a rapidly growing digital advertising network — three category-defining businesses stitched together by an operational obsession with customer experience and relentless long-term reinvestment.

AWS (Amazon Web Services): $128.7B revenue, 24% growth (fastest in 13 quarters), ~38% operating margin, ~57% of total operating income. Powers the AI economy — from startups to the Pentagon. Hosts Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI, and 100,000+ enterprise customers on Bedrock.

Advertising Services: $68.6B revenue (+22%), second-most profitable segment. Built on 300M+ customer purchase intent signals — the highest-quality ad targeting data on earth. Growing faster than Meta or Google within its TAM.

Retail (NA + International): $588.2B revenue. Low-margin (~3-4% operating) but generates the customer data flywheel and network effects that make all three segments compoundingly valuable.

March 2026 development: Amazon acquired Fauna Robotics (humanoid robots) and announced a $200B capex plan for 2026 — the single largest capital commitment in corporate history, focused almost entirely on AI infrastructure.

Economic Moat Assessment
AWS Cloud Switching Costs — Extraordinary
Migrating off AWS is measured in years, not months. Customers integrate AWS services (200+ services) deeply into architecture. Re-training internal teams, re-architecting applications, negotiating new contracts — the total cost of switching is frequently 3-5× the annual AWS bill. Bedrock adds a second layer: AI models fine-tuned on AWS cannot be portably moved.
★★★★★
Logistics Network — Structural Cost Advantage
1.5M+ robots, 1,000+ fulfilment centers, last-mile delivery infrastructure built over 20 years. Amazon's cost-per-delivery is lower than UPS/FedEx on dense routes. The network is too expensive to replicate — any competitor would need $100B+ and 10 years to build equivalent density. AI route optimization is making the cost gap wider, not narrower.
★★★★★
Purchase Intent Data Flywheel — Advertising Moat
300M+ active customers. Amazon knows WHAT you searched for, what you clicked on, what you added to cart, and what you ultimately bought. This is the highest-quality advertising signal available — purchase intent is more valuable than any social signal. Advertisers pay premium CPMs for Amazon's targeting data. No competitor can replicate this without 20 years of purchase history.
★★★★★
Prime Ecosystem Lock-in
~200M+ Prime subscribers globally. Benefits stack: free delivery + Prime Video + Prime Music + Prime Reading + Prime Gaming + exclusive deals + early access. The more services used, the more sticky the subscription. Prime members spend ~3-4× more on Amazon than non-Prime members. Annual price raised 17% in 2023 with minimal churn — proof of pricing power.
★★★★★
3P Marketplace Network Effect
3.2M+ active sellers. More sellers → more selection → more customers → more sellers. Amazon's marketplace is the world's largest product discovery engine. 60%+ of units sold are third-party. Seller services (fulfilment, advertising, lending) are high-margin and growing faster than retail. Sellers cannot easily leave because Amazon IS where their customers are.
★★★★☆

Trainium2: 40% more cost-effective than NVIDIA H100 for LLM training. Inferentia: designed for inference at scale. As Trainium's market share grows, AWS can offer lower-cost AI compute while maintaining margins. This is a structural cost advantage vs rivals still dependent on NVIDIA GPUs. Trainium3 is ramping, Trainium4 expected 2027 with 6× compute improvement.
