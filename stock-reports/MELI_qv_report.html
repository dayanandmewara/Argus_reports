Business Overview

MercadoLibre, founded in 1999 and listed on NASDAQ, is Latin America's dominant e-commerce and fintech platform operating across 18 countries, with Brazil (~60% revenue), Mexico (~20%), and Argentina (~10%) as primary markets. The company operates five interconnected flywheels: Mercado Libre Marketplace (e-commerce), Mercado Pago (payments + digital banking), Mercado Credito (lending), Mercado Envios (logistics), and Mercado Ads (advertising). Each segment feeds the others: payments data improves credit underwriting; logistics scale lowers seller costs; credit availability lifts GMV; advertising revenue grows with GMV scale. New CEO Ariel Szarfsztejn (from Jan 1, 2026; founder Marcos Galperin now Executive Chairman) is accelerating the logistics offensive in Brazil with a $10.9 billion investment in 2026 (+50% YoY).

Why This Stock Is Interesting Right Now

After reporting Q1 2026 results (May 7, 2026), MELI stock fell 11–13% to a 52-week low despite the strongest revenue growth in four years (+49%). This is a classic "market punishing acceleration" scenario where GAAP accounting (mandatory upfront provisioning for the entire expected life of new loans) creates an optical earnings miss. The underlying business — 84M active buyers, $87B TPV, $19B GMV — is healthier than any point in its history. Two-thirds of margin compression is accounting convention, not economic deterioration. At $1,575, the stock trades at ~11x annualised FCF and ~2.3x annualised forward revenue — multiples rarely available for a 49%-growth franchise with a confirmed data flywheel.

🔴 WHY MELI FELL 11–13% AFTER Q1 2026 RESULTS — DIAGNOSTIC

1
EPS Miss of 12.2%: $8.23 reported vs $9.37 consensus — the biggest single driver of the sell-off. High-multiple growth stocks are binary on EPS; a 12% miss triggers algorithmic selling. However, the entire miss is explained by credit provisioning (see #2) and logistics investment, not any deterioration in core commerce or payment economics.
2
Credit Book Provisioning (Accounting, Not Economics): MELI's credit book grew 87% YoY to $14.6B. GAAP requires provisioning for the full expected loss of every new loan at origination. When the book grows faster than revenues (87% vs 49%), provisions front-load pain — creating a 600bps operating margin compression to 6.9%. CFO Martin De Los Santos was explicit: "Two-thirds of the margin compression comes from credit provisioning. That is natural." Net Interest Margin After Losses (NIMAL) remained healthy at 36.2%.
3
Free Shipping Threshold Cut in Brazil: MELI deliberately lowered Brazil's free shipping threshold to gain market share — boosting items sold by 56% but compressing near-term margins. This is an offensive investment already showing results: Brazil GMV grew 38%, unique buyers in Brazil rose by a record 17 million YoY.
4
Institutional Selling Post-Results: WCM Investment Management filed an SEC disclosure showing they sold approximately $940M of MELI stock (248K shares). WCM had been a long-term holder. Separately, Daiwa downgraded to Hold and multiple analysts reduced price targets. These mechanical selling events depressed the price further.
5
Pre-Existing Technical Weakness: MELI had already fallen 30%+ from its June 2025 high of $2,645 before Q1 results, as the market fretted about margin trajectory. With the stock already weak and below all key moving averages, the EPS miss became a catalyst for further forced selling to all-time 52-week lows.
6
No Margin Guidance: Management declined to provide specific margin recovery guidance or timing, creating uncertainty. UBS (downgraded to Neutral, PT $2,050) argues margin recovery is 2027 at earliest. This ambiguity allows bears to project continued compression with no clear catalyst to close the gap.
