Business Overview

Persistent Systems is an AI-led digital engineering and enterprise modernisation company, founded by Dr. Anand Deshpande in 1990. It serves over 350 clients — including 20 of the Fortune 50 — primarily in the United States, across three verticals: Banking, Financial Services & Insurance (BFSI) at ~34%, Healthcare & Life Sciences (HLS) at ~26%, and Technology, Media & Emerging Verticals at ~40%.

The business model is centred on deep engineering partnerships: selling skilled engineering time to enterprise clients, with AI platforms (ERAG, iAURA) increasingly enabling productivity-driven, outcome-based delivery — shifting from pure headcount growth toward intellectual leverage.

In June 2026, Persistent announced the acquisition of Nagarro SE (Germany) at EUR 81/share (~140% premium), creating a combined entity with annualised revenue of ~$2.9B and 46,000+ employees across 40+ countries. This transformative deal adds European scale, SAP/ERP capabilities, and ~€1B in additional revenue. VERIFIED

BQV Series Rationale

Why analyse now? Persistent has corrected ~34% from its 52-week high of ₹6,599 to ₹4,350 as of today, driven by sector de-rating, FII outflows, and Nagarro acquisition uncertainty. The question is whether this pullback offers an entry into a compounding machine or whether headwinds justify continued caution.

Why Cat B? 4-year USD revenue CAGR of 25.6%, growing ROIC (26%→34%), top-decile margins for a services company, and a large, underpenetrated digital engineering TAM. Revenue CAGR far exceeds the Cat C threshold.

Framework adaptation: Persistent straddles the IT Services (THREAT) and IT Products/SaaS (WIDENER) categories. We classify it as 🟢 WIDENER with FORMING flywheel — the AI platform strategy (ERAG, iAURA) creates genuine productivity leverage, but the core model remains people-intensive. This tension is explicitly noted.
