Business Overview
What Nu Holdings Does

Nu Holdings (NYSE: NU), operating as Nubank, is the world's largest digital bank by customer count. Founded in São Paulo in 2013 by David Vélez (Colombian), Cristina Junqueira, and Edward Wible, Nu disrupted Brazil's entrenched, fee-heavy banking oligopoly with a zero-fee, app-only credit card. It has since expanded into a full financial super-app offering: credit cards, personal accounts, personal loans, investments, insurance, SME banking, NuPay (payments), NuCel (mobile), and the NuMarketplace. It listed on NYSE in December 2021 at ~$9/share.


As of December 2025: 131 million customers globally — 113M in Brazil (62% of adult population), ~15M in Mexico (15% of adults, #1 issuer of new credit cards), and 4M+ in Colombia. Brazil contributes ~85%+ of revenue. Mexico and Colombia are in early but accelerating phases.

Why This Stock Is In The Series

Nu is the MercadoLibre of digital banking — a generational platform disruption in an underbanked 650M-person market. It combines:
• Data flywheel: 600B+ proprietary tokens → nuFormer model → 3x better credit decisions
• Network effects: larger customer base → better data → more products → deeper engagement
• Structural cost moat: $0.80/month cost to serve vs $15-25 for traditional banks
• ARPAC expansion runway: currently $15/month vs $50-80 at mature banks

The AI angle is confirmed, not speculative — nuFormer is live in production credit decisions.
