Business Overview
What They Do

Enviro Infra Engineers Ltd (EIEL), incorporated 2009, designs, constructs, operates, and maintains Water Treatment Plants (WTPs), Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs), and Common Effluent Treatment Plants (CETPs) for government bodies. Primary clients: urban local bodies, state water boards, industrial estates. Revenue split: EPC construction (~80%) + O&M recurring (~20%). ESTIMATED

Why This Stock Now

EIEL sits at the intersection of three megatrends: India's chronic water stress (per capita availability fell from 1,800 m³ in 2001 → 1,400 m³ in 2025), JJM 2.0 budget surge (4× increase in FY27), and AMRUT-driven STP mandates. After a 43% correction from Dec 2024 ATH, market asks: is growth real or guidance fiction? The ₹6,814 Cr order book is the answer's raw material — execution is the test. VERIFIED

ROIC as Moat Proof

Per framework: 15–20% ROIC = narrow moat. EIEL's ROIC has declined from 64% (FY22) to 20% (FY26). The current 20% is barely above the narrow-moat threshold. The trajectory — not the level — is the concern: each year sees lower ROIC despite growing scale. This is typical of an EPC business entering a new growth phase where invested capital grows faster than returns.

Moat Verdict: B– (Narrow, Declining)

EIEL has a narrow, real moat in technical execution, CRISIL A creditworthiness, and government relationships — but it is EPC-dependent, not a monopoly. ROIC declining, FCF negative, competitive bids. A "wide moat" claim would be incorrect.
