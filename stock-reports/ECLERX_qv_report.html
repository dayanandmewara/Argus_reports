Business Overview

What eClerx Does

eClerx Services is a Mumbai-headquartered Knowledge Process Outsourcing (KPO) and digital operations firm serving Fortune 500 enterprises across financial services, telecom, cable, retail/fashion, media & entertainment, manufacturing, and technology. Incorporated in 2000, the company has grown into a globally distributed business with ~22,600 employees across 17 countries. It is listed on both BSE (532927) and NSE (ECLERX).

Core service lines: Financial Services Ops (capital markets, wealth management, compliance) · Cable & Telecom (subscriber analytics, billing ops) · Digital Operations (content services, customer experience) · Analytics & Automation (now a USD 90mn book) · Market360 (digital shelf analytics).

Revenue geography: North America 78% · Europe 17% · RoW 5%. Client tenure is deep — top-10 concentration reduced to 59% (from higher) and 24 clients now contribute >$3mn revenue (vs 18 in FY25), indicating healthy diversification.

Series Rationale & Framework Adaptation

eClerx is classified as Category C — Mature Quality Compounder. It is not a structurally premium Indian consumer monopoly (Category A) nor an AI-native software firm (Category F). It occupies the interesting middle ground: a domain-specialist BPO/KPO with genuine switching costs, above-average ROIC, and a credible AI-augmentation strategy that could structurally widen margins and deepen client relationships. The AI Overlay is 🟢 WIDENER because its 25 years of domain-specific transaction data (financial services workflows, telecom billing records, digital shelf analytics) represents a proprietary training corpus that commodity AI cannot replicate. However, the flywheel is FORMING — not yet CONFIRMED — because independent evidence of a self-reinforcing AI improvement loop is not yet reflected in public disclosures. Framework adaptation: Standard Cat C DCF + owner earnings methodology applies; the AI Widener adds a modest +2–5% terminal growth premium and +3–5x terminal P/E justification. Key risk: AI deflationary pressure on legacy BPO volumes (management acknowledged this in Q4 FY26 call but expressed confidence in top-quartile growth).
