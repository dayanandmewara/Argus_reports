Business Overview

Adani Power Limited (APL) is India's largest private sector thermal power producer, incorporated in 1996 and publicly listed in 2009. It operates 17 GW of installed capacity across Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Jharkhand, and Tamil Nadu. The flagship Mundra thermal plant in Gujarat (4.62 GW) is India's largest single-site coal-fired plant and one of the largest in the world. VERIFIED

Revenue generation is split between long-term Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) with state electricity boards (~70-75% of sales), medium/short-term PPAs, and merchant power sales at spot market tariffs. This mix provides base revenue stability from PPAs while offering margin upside when merchant tariffs spike during peak demand periods.

Category Classification & Framework Rationale

Category C — Mature Quality Compounder (Infrastructure Variant). Adani Power does not fit FMCG (no consumer brand), Category B (not a consumer-internet penetration story), or Category D (turnaround is complete — profitable for 4 consecutive years). It fits Category C best: large-scale infrastructure operator with durable, multi-decade PPA contracts as its core moat, ROIC improving (though still below the 20% threshold due to capital intensity inherent to thermal power), and a clear 5-year growth plan anchored by contracted capacity.

The framework adaptation for utilities: EV/EBITDA is more relevant than P/E given heavy D&A from long-lived assets. FCF is constrained by the CapEx expansion phase. Owner Earnings (replacing growth CapEx with maintenance-only) is the cleaner earnings measure. Terminal multiple is appropriate for a regulated-infrastructure compounder with 25-year PPAs providing revenue visibility.

Economic Moat Assessment
Long-Term PPA Contracts (Switching Cost)★★★★
25-year PPAs with state electricity boards lock in revenue and create regulatory switching costs. DISCOMs cannot easily replace a 17 GW baseload provider. APL has recently secured a landmark 1,600 MW, 25-year PPA with MSEDCL (Maharashtra). The regulatory resolution of all legacy disputes removes legacy overhang and strengthens long-term PPA credibility. VERIFIED
Scale & Coal Logistics Advantage★★★
17 GW of scale provides significant cost advantages in coal procurement, logistics, and operations. Mundra plant (4.62 GW, world's 3rd largest single-site thermal) benefits from captive port access. APL operates its own coal supply chain, reducing dependence on third parties. Replicable by NTPC at equal scale — moat is real but not monopolistic. ESTIMATED
Ultra-Supercritical Technology Premium★★★
APL is deploying ultra-supercritical (USC) technology in new plants, achieving the lowest per-unit carbon emissions in India's thermal sector. This creates a regulatory compliance moat — as environmental standards tighten, APL's USC plants outcompete older subcritical assets. Advance orders placed for 11.2 GW of USC equipment. VERIFIED
Regulatory & Execution Experience★★★
17 years of operating large thermal assets across 8 states. All pending regulatory matters resolved as of FY25 — including carrying costs and late payment surcharges from DISCOMs. This resolution (contributing to FY23/24 one-time income) demonstrates institutional expertise in navigating India's complex power sector regulation. New entrants would spend years in the same disputes. VERIFIED
IBC Acquisition Capability (Distressed Asset Moat)★★
APL has demonstrated ability to acquire distressed power assets through the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code at significant discounts (Coastal Energen 1.2 GW at ₹33.4 bn; Lanco Amarkantak 0.6+1.32 GW at ₹41 bn). This is a capacity addition channel not available to non-listed competitors. Moat is size-dependent — only works at scale. VERIFIED
⚠ Moat Threat — Long-term Renewable Disruption★★
Solar and wind tariffs continue to fall. Renewables met 51.5% of India's 203 GW demand in July 2025. By 2030-32, new baseload thermal plants may face lower merchant tariffs as RE + storage improves. APL's 15-20 year PPA cover provides protection, but merchant segment (25-30% of revenue) is exposed. APL is responding by adding solar and hydro exposure. VERIFIED
ROIC as Moat Proof (5-Year Trend)

ROIC for infrastructure companies requires context. At 9-12% ROIC vs ~12% WACC, APL is at the breakeven margin — not a wide-moat company on ROIC alone. However, ROIC is capital-intensity constrained (not management-quality constrained), and the return on incremental capital (new USC plants at better tariffs) is improving. The correct frame is: does the CAPACITY EXPANSION create value at above-WACC returns? Evidence suggests yes — new PPAs are being signed at tariffs that generate ~15-18% project IRRs (industry benchmark).
