Business Overview

Shadowfax Technologies (NSE: SHADOWFAX) is India's second-largest third-party express logistics platform by volume, delivering 20+ crore shipments per quarter across express parcel, hyperlocal/quick-commerce, and on-demand mobility. Founded 2015 in Bangalore by Abhishek Bansal and Vaibhav Khandelwal, it listed on NSE/BSE on January 28, 2026, raising ₹1,907 Cr via IPO at ₹124/share. The company is asset-light (all facilities leased), operates a crowdsourced delivery partner model, and covers 15,166 PIN codes across 2,300+ cities. Revenue mix: ~75% Express Parcel, ~20% Hyperlocal/Quick-Commerce, ~5% Other Logistics Services (OLS including CriticaLog acquisition).

Classification Rationale
Why Category B

Revenue CAGR FY23–25: +32%; accelerating to +67% in 9M FY26. Underpenetrated market (India's e-commerce logistics at only 7% online retail penetration vs 25%+ in China). ROIC still building — GAAP profits only emerged in FY25. Large TAM: India express logistics projected 19–23% shipment CAGR to FY30. Asset-light model with operating leverage thesis. Classic Cat B profile: growth outrunning GAAP earnings due to heavy investment phase.

Why AI Neutral ⚪

Shadowfax deploys AI for route optimisation (SF Maps), fraud detection (SF Shield), order batching, and address intelligence. However, these are operational efficiency tools also deployed by competitor Delhivery. No proprietary data flywheel with measurable AI improvement metrics published. AI enhances unit economics but does not create a structural moat unavailable to competitors. Classification: AI is a tool, not a moat.

Why This Stock is in the Series

Shadowfax represents a pure-play on India's logistics modernisation — specifically the explosive confluence of e-commerce penetration and quick commerce growth. It is the only publicly listed, 100% last-mile focused, tech-driven 3PL at scale after Ecom Express's exit from the listed space. With 65%+ revenue growth at IPO-age, EBITDA expanding 270 bps YoY, and coverage of India's 2nd-largest logistics network, it merits systematic analysis. The key analytical challenge: can this business, with razor-thin 4.3% adj EBITDA margins and extreme client concentration, compound ROIC above cost of capital over a decade? That is the Buffett question.

Critical Moat Risk: Client Concentration

⚠ The #1 Moat Vulnerability: Flipkart Dependence

A logistics company where 48–59% of revenue comes from a single client is structurally NOT a wide-moat business by Buffett standards. If Flipkart (owned by Walmart) decides to expand its captive logistics arm (Ekart) or shifts volume to Delhivery, Shadowfax loses its moat overnight. This is the antithesis of the "castle and moat" metaphor — the castle's survival depends entirely on the king's goodwill. The moat assessment is capped at B- until top-client concentration falls below 35%.
