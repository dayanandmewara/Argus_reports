Business Description

Incorporated in 1988 and headquartered in Kolkata, Laser Power & Infra Limited is an integrated manufacturer of power cables, conductors and specialised components for India's power transmission and distribution sector, operating through two segments: Manufacturing (~73% of FY26 revenue — LV/MV power cables, aerial bunched cables, control & quad cables, specialty cables, aluminium rods, PVC compounds, and conductors including ACSR) and EPC (~27% of FY26 revenue — turnkey power distribution projects: rural/urban electrification, substations, transmission lines). The manufacturing division supplies both third parties and the company's own EPC arm, an integrated model management calls a cost and quality advantage. The company operates three manufacturing units in West Bengal (two in Dhulagarh, one in Kharagpur) with combined installed capacity in the 73,100–85,448 MT range ESTIMATED — sources vary, is an RDSO-accredited registered supplier to Indian Railways, has completed 43+ EPC projects across 26 states, 4 union territories and 10 countries, and holds a manufacturing partnership with US-based transmission technology firm TS Conductor Corp. It is also diversifying into solar, battery energy storage, and water pipeline projects.

Series Rationale — Why This Stock

This report exists to stress-test a live, actively-marketed mainboard IPO (₹742 crore; fresh issue ₹542 cr + OFS ₹200 cr) against the same quality-value discipline applied to listed compounders — precisely because IPO marketing materials and brokerage "Subscribe" calls tend to foreground growth narrative (order book +49% FY24–26, sector CAGR of 11–13%, de-leveraging use of proceeds) while underweighting cash-flow quality. What Makes This Challenging

The core problem

FY26 revenue declined 9.4% while EBITDA margin and PAT rose — an unusual combination that resolves once you see receivables ballooned by ₹357 cr in one year (DSO 135→196 days) and operating cash flow went negative ₹119 cr against a positive ₹60 cr the year before, in the same year the company booked a ₹32.79 cr one-time gain from selling a subsidiary.

The counter-argument

The IPO prices at ~19.8–25.3x FY26 earnings post-issue versus large peers (Polycab, KEI, Havells) at 45–65x, with ₹490 cr of the fresh issue earmarked for debt repayment that should mechanically lower interest costs and could improve reported quality going forward.

This coincided with operating cash flow turning negative ₹119 cr (from +₹60 cr in FY25) as receivables grew ₹357 cr in a single year.
