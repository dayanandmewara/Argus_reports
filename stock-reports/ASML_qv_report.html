Business Overview

ASML Holding N.V. is the Dutch semiconductor equipment company that has achieved one of the most extraordinary monopolies in modern industry: it is the sole manufacturer of Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines on earth. No company — not Applied Materials, not Lam Research, not TSMC itself — can produce the advanced chips that power AI, smartphones, and data centres without ASML's equipment.

EUV machines produce light by firing a high-powered laser at droplets of molten tin 50,000 times per second to generate wavelengths of 13.5 nanometres — shorter than any competitor can achieve. This physics breakthrough, 20+ years in development, lets chipmakers etch transistors below 5nm. Every 3nm chip (Apple M-series, NVIDIA H100/H200/B200, AMD MI300X) was made on ASML EUV machines. VERIFIED

The company's installed base of 4,000+ machines across TSMC, Samsung, Intel, and SK Hynix creates a recurring service and upgrade revenue stream (Installed Base Management = €8.2B in 2025, up 26% YoY). ASML is headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands, employs 44,000+ people, and is listed on both Euronext Amsterdam and NASDAQ.

Why This Stock Is in the Series

ASML represents a rare convergence: an existing wide-moat business (Category C compounder) whose moat is being actively widened by the AI revolution, not disrupted by it. Every dollar spent on AI training or inference requires more advanced chips, which require more ASML machines. The company is the ultimate AI infrastructure play — without ASML, there is no NVIDIA H100, no AI data centre, no large language model.

The key investment challenge: The quality is unimpeachable. The moat is A+. The question is purely about price. At ~50x trailing P/E — 40% above its 10-year average of 36x — the stock prices in a lot of perfection. The China revenue normalization (33%→20%) is a near-term headwind. The entry zone of ~$1,050–$1,200 would represent a margin of safety worth acting on.

Catalysts to watch: (1) Q1 2026 earnings tomorrow — order intake, gross margin trajectory, China normalization commentary; (2) High-NA EUV ramp — at $400M+ per unit, these are the next decade's growth engine; (3) MATCH Act passage risk — new US legislation that could further restrict DUV sales to China.

YES ✓ — ASML's machines collect real-time yield, overlay, and process data from 4,000+ installations at TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix globally. This is the most comprehensive semiconductor process performance dataset in existence. No other company has access to this breadth of fab performance data across all the world's top chipmakers simultaneously. FORMING ✓ — ASML invested €1.3B in Mistral AI in 2025 specifically to accelerate "AI-driven innovation in holistic lithography and operations." FORMING ✓ — The analysis from Klover.ai (Aug 2025) notes that once a customer's fab processes have been "learned" and optimised by ASML's AI agents, "the cost, time, and risk associated with switching to a hypothetical competitor — which would have to begin the entire learning process from scratch — become astronomical." This is a genuine compounding switching cost. However, since ASML is already a monopolist with 100% EUV share, the loop "better AI → more customers" operates more as retention and IBM revenue expansion than new customer acquisition. The flywheel deepens economic relationships with existing customers rather than expanding the customer base. ESTIMATED
