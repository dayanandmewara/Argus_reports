BUSINESS OVERVIEW

HBL Engineering Limited (formerly HBL Power Systems) is a 45-year-old Hyderabad-based defence and industrial technology company operating across four verticals: Industrial Batteries, Defence & Aviation Batteries, Electronics (Kavach/TCAS/TMS), and Emerging Technology. Founded in 1977 as Hyderabad Batteries Ltd and merged in 1999, the company has transformed from a battery manufacturer into a defence-electronics-and-power solutions conglomerate under founder Dr. Aluru Jagadish Prasad.

The electronics segment — specifically Kavach (ATP system), TCAS, TMS, and CTC — is now the primary growth engine. HBL holds a quasi-monopoly position in supplying Kavach systems to Indian Railways, which has become a national mandate for anti-collision technology across its entire ~67,000 km network. Combined with a global #2 position in Ni-Cad batteries, this creates an unusually durable competitive position for a mid-cap industrial.

WHY THIS STOCK — SERIES RATIONALE
STRUCTURAL TAILWIND
India's Kavach mandate covers ~67,000 km. HBL is the only RDSO-approved supplier at scale. This is a 5–7 year infrastructure spend — secular not cyclical.
DEFENCE INDIGENISATION
Atmanirbhar Bharat policy favours domestic suppliers in defence batteries (submarines, missiles, torpedoes) where HBL is the only Indian maker of several niche products.
BALANCE SHEET STRENGTH
Net cash ₹508 Cr, D/E 0.04x, debt-free effectively. This is a rare combination with government contract dependency — enables order execution without leverage risk.
KEY RISK: ORDER LUMPINESS
FY25 was a -12% revenue year due to RDSO delays. FY26 FY26 Q4 margins collapsed. Government ordering is lumpy — execution risk is real and creates volatile quarterly earnings.
WORKING CAPITAL INTENSITY
Large Kavach contracts (₹1,500+ Cr CLW order) require significant upfront capital. FCF conversion in FY26 was only ~27% of PAT — a structural headwind in high-growth years.
POST-KAVACH QUESTION
Kavach build-out expected to taper after FY28-29. Management is building TMS, CTC, Fuzes, Lithium defence batteries — but replacement revenue scale and timing remain uncertain.

ECONOMIC MOAT ANALYSIS
1. REGULATORY MOAT — RDSO APPROVALS & DEFENCE CERTIFICATIONS
RDSO (Research Designs & Standards Organisation) certification for Kavach/TCAS takes years to obtain and cannot be fast-tracked. HBL is one of only 2–3 RDSO-approved Kavach suppliers at scale and the only one for on-board Kavach at locomotive level (CLW contract). Defence certifications for submarine batteries, torpedo thermal batteries, and aviation batteries create near-impenetrable barriers. New entrants face 3–5 years of certification timelines.
