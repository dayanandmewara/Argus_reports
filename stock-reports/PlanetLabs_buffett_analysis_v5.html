Business Model — Imaging the Entire Earth Every Day

What Planet does: Founded in 2010 by three NASA scientists (Will Marshall, Robbie Schingler, Chris Boshuizen), Planet operates the largest commercial Earth observation fleet — 200+ satellites providing daily, global coverage of the entire Earth's landmass. It sells access to this data (subscriptions, APIs, analytics) and, increasingly, sells AI-powered insights derived from this data. Customers: defence/intelligence agencies, civil governments, agriculture companies, insurers, environmental organizations, financial institutions.

Revenue model: 97–98% of Annual Contract Value (ACV) is recurring — subscription-based access to daily imagery streams, multi-year satellite services contracts, and ongoing analytics subscriptions. This is closer to SaaS economics than hardware. NRR was 116% in FY22; management no longer reports exact NRR but the 97–98% ACV recurring metric and backlog growth confirm healthy cohort dynamics.

Three constellation tiers: (1) PlanetScope / SuperDove — daily 3.7m resolution global monitoring, the core data subscription; (2) SkySat / Pelican — high-resolution (0.3–0.5m) tasking for specific targets; (3) Tanager — hyperspectral for methane/carbon monitoring (regulatory compliance demand). New: Owl — next-gen monitoring at 1m class, due 2027.

Why this is truly Category F: The 15-year daily archive is not just data — it is the TRAINING CORPUS for every Earth-observing AI model that will ever be built. Models trained on Planet's archive can detect deforestation, predict crop yields, identify ship movements, assess battle damage, and measure methane leaks — because they have the temporal comparison that comes from having a photograph of every square kilometer of the planet for 15 consecutive years. This temporal depth is structurally impossible to replicate — you cannot go back in time to image the Earth in 2015. Planet's archive is permanent, growing, and singular.
