Business Overview

Tata Power is a 100+ year old, vertically integrated power company spanning generation (thermal, hydro, solar, wind), transmission, distribution, solar manufacturing and EV charging. V Transmission & Distribution has grown from ~59% of revenue (FY22) to ~62% (9M FY25), and the company runs distribution franchises serving 12.5 million customers across Mumbai, Delhi (via TPDDL), Odisha (TPCODL) and Ajmer (TPADL), alongside a 4,633-ckm transmission network. V

Its renewable arm, Tata Power Renewable Energy Ltd (TPREL), targets roughly 23 GW of renewable capacity by FY30 as part of a company-wide goal of 70% renewable generation capacity (and ~31 GW total capacity) by 2030 — up from a 43% green portfolio today. V Solar cell/module manufacturing capacity spans Bengaluru and a 4.3 GW plant in Tirunelveli, Tamil Nadu, and the company has built India's largest rooftop-solar installed base under its retail push. V

Why Category D

The framework's Category A–C buckets require ROIC consistently above 15–20% with a durable, demonstrated moat. Tata Power's ROCE has run at 10–11% for the past four years V — below even the "narrow moat" threshold — while free cash flow has been negative and deepening (₹‑4,357 Cr FY25 → ₹‑7,677 Cr FY26) V as the company funds a multi-year renewable build-out mostly with debt (borrowings up 21% YoY to ₹76,141 Cr). V That combination — sub-threshold current profitability metrics, an explicit multi-year transformation thesis, and event risk around execution (Q4 FY26 PAT missed estimates and the stock fell ~11% over five sessions) V — is the textbook definition of the framework's Category D, not a steady-state quality compounder.

Why AI Overlay = WIDENER (mild)

Tata Power is not an AI company, but AI intersects its business in two real ways: (1) data-centre and industrial electrification is becoming a structural new source of power demand, and management has pointed to grid-transformation capex as partly tied to this trend; V and (2) the company has partnered with Databricks to build a data/AI platform for grid operations and predictive maintenance. V Neither rises to a proprietary data flywheel — see Tab 3 and Tab 9 — so this is graded a mild, not a structural, widener.

What makes this name interesting — and what makes it hard
  • Interesting: A AAA-pedigree Tata Group balance sheet (CRISIL AA+/Stable) V executing one of India's largest private coal-to-clean pivots, with steadily rising domestic institutional ownership (DIIs up from 14.2% to 18.0% of the register over three years) V even as the stock has been flat-to-down over one year.
  • Hard: Earnings quality has genuinely deteriorated this year — operating cash flow conversion fell from 109% of operating profit (FY25) to 55% (FY26) V — and sell-side views on fair value are unusually dispersed: Motilal Oswal's ₹490 Buy target sits against Morgan Stanley's ₹399 Equal-weight and at least one foreign broker's ₹300 Sell call. V That spread tells you the market hasn't agreed on how much of the renewable story is already in the price.

Method 2/3 — Owner Earnings Capitalisation & ROIC/EPV

Economic Profit = (ROIC − WACC) × Invested Capital. With ROIC at ~10–11% against a Category-D WACC of 14–16%, this spread is currently negative — on a trailing basis, incremental capital deployed is not yet clearing its cost of capital. E This is the single most important valuation finding in this report: the bull case is not "the business earns excess returns today," it is "the business will earn excess returns once ~23GW of renewable capacity is fully ramped and debt-funded capex moderates post-2028." That is a real but unproven thesis, not a current fact.
