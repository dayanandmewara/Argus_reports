Business Snapshot

Wipro Limited (est. 1945, transformed IT-first post-2000) is India's 3rd largest IT services company by revenue, offering technology consulting, cloud, cybersecurity, data/AI, engineering R&D and BPS to 65+ countries. Revenue mix: BFSI ~33%, Consumer ~15%, Manufacturing ~10%, Healthcare ~10%, Energy & Utilities ~8%, others. Geographically, Americas ~55%, Europe ~28%, APAC+Others ~17%. Under CEO Srini Pallia (since Apr 2024), the company has pivoted to an "AI-first, consulting-led" model via the Wipro ai360 ecosystem ($1B investment), Lab45 R&D unit with 50+ Agentic AI solutions, and restructured business into 4 segments: Technology Services, BPS, Consulting, and Engineering & R&D from FY26.

FY26 marked a tentative revenue recovery (+4.0% YoY in INR) after two muted years, though in constant currency terms IT services revenue was flat to slightly negative. Large deal TCV momentum improved (Q1FY26 TCV surged 130% YoY) but deal ramp-up is uneven. The AI revenue contribution is still ~5% of total but growing fast from a low base.

Series Rationale — Why Cover Wipro Now?

Three reasons this stock demands attention right now:

1. Buyback Arbitrage Opportunity: Wipro has announced a ₹15,000 Cr tender buyback at ₹250/share. The stock currently trades at ~₹193 — representing a 29.5% premium. Retail (small shareholder) allocation offers attractive risk/reward especially across multiple accounts.

2. AI Existential Question: The sector is at an inflection — GenAI and Agentic AI are compressing the unit economics of traditional IT services (2–3% annual revenue deflation expected). Is Wipro building a credible AI-native moat, or is it at risk of being disrupted from below?

3. Valuation Floor Being Tested: At 15x P/E (near a decade low), FY26 earnings recovery (+0.5% PAT), cash-rich balance sheet, and a ₹250 buyback signal, the stock is optically cheap. The framework question is whether this cheapness reflects deserved re-rating or temporary overshoot.

Moat Sources Analysis

1. Switching Costs (Client Lock-in)
★★★½
Multi-year enterprise contracts, deep system integration, and the cost/risk of migrating large ERP/cloud workloads creates meaningful switching friction. Average contract tenure 3–5 years. However, AI-native challengers offering 40–60% cost reduction are beginning to shorten re-tender cycles. Stress test: HOLDS in base case, WEAKENS if Agentic AI adoption accelerates faster than expected.
2. Scale & Delivery Network
★★★
220,000+ employees, global delivery centers, 65+ country presence. Scale allows competitive pricing on large deals (>$500M TCV). However, large headcount is becoming a cost liability rather than an asset in an AI-driven world. Stress test: WEAKENS — scale moat inverts if AI reduces headcount requirements.
3. Client Relationships & Domain Expertise (BFSI, ER&D)
★★★★
BFSI deep expertise (33% revenue), regulatory knowledge, long-standing tier-1 banking/insurance relationships. Engineering R&D expertise hard to replicate. This is the most durable part of Wipro's moat — AI cannot easily replace sector-specific regulatory and process knowledge. Stress test: STRENGTHENS as clients need trusted advisors for AI implementation governance.
4. Intangible Assets (Brand, IP, Certifications)
★★★
Top-5 Indian IT brand, ISO certifications, Wipro ai360 IP, Lab45 patents. Brand matters in large enterprise deal shortlists. However, brand alone does not command premium pricing. Stress test: HOLDS.
5. Cost Advantage (India Labour Arbitrage)
★★
India talent cost advantage (30–40% vs Western IT) has been the foundational moat for 25 years. This moat is being directly eroded by AI automation — GenAI code generation reduces junior developer requirements by 20–40%. Stress test: WEAKENS materially over 5-7 year horizon.
