Company Classification & Why It's In The Series

Category B — High-Growth Global Compounder. SoFi is a digital bank and fintech superapp targeting financially-savvy young professionals (HENRYs — High Earners Not Rich Yet). Founded 2011, went public via SPAC in 2021. The pivotal structural event was obtaining an OCC national bank charter in February 2022, allowing SoFi to hold deposits and fund loans at ~4–5% cost (vs 7–9% warehouse lines for non-bank fintechs). Revenue has compounded at ~45% CAGR over five years (FY2020–FY2025 adjusted), driven by three segments: Lending (personal loans, student loans, home loans), Financial Services (banking, invest, credit card, crypto), and Technology Platform (Galileo API infrastructure + Technisys core banking — serving 160M+ enabled accounts for fintechs globally).

Why in the series: SoFi sits at a critical inflection — first full year of GAAP profitability (FY2024), bank charter lowering funding costs, Galileo creating a B2B moat, and stock down 50% from all-time high. However a serious short-seller report from Muddy Waters (March 2026) alleges accounting manipulation, inflated EBITDA, and understated charge-offs. Resolution of this cloud is the central question for the investment thesis.

⚠ CRITICAL RISK — Muddy Waters Short Report (March 17, 2026)
Carson Block's Muddy Waters Research (MW) published a 28-page short report titled "SOFI: A Financial Engineering Treadmill." Key allegations: (1) FY2025 Adjusted EBITDA inflated by ~90% — true EBITDA ~$103M vs $1.05B reported; (2) $312M unrecorded debt via a JPMorgan loan transaction booked as a "sale" but allegedly secured borrowing per Utah UCC filings; (3) Personal loan charge-off rate understated at 2.89% vs estimated actual of ~6.1% (allegedly defaulted loans parked in unconsolidated entities); (4) Management bonus structure incentivizes fair value manipulation; (5) CEO and CFO extracted $58.3M via prepaid variable forward contracts (prearranged stock sales).

SoFi's response: Denied all allegations, called the report "factually inaccurate and misleading," threatened legal action. CEO Noto bought $500K of stock on open market. Mizuho analyst Don Dolev pushed back substantively on key claims. SoFi did NOT answer 11 specific factual questions posed by Muddy Waters. Short interest rose to 10.1% (multi-month high). UNRESOLVED
