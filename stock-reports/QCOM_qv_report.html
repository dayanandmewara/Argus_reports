Business Overview

What Qualcomm Does

Qualcomm is the world's leading fabless semiconductor design and IP-licensing company operating three segments. QCT (CDMA Technologies) — chip design — provides Snapdragon SoCs for smartphones, automotive (Snapdragon Digital Chassis), IoT, PCs, and now data centres under the new Dragonfly brand. QTL (Technology Licensing) monetises the planet's largest 3G/4G/5G standard-essential patent portfolio at ~70%+ margins. QSI (Strategic Initiatives) is a small venture arm.

Yesterday (June 24, 2026), Qualcomm executed the most ambitious strategic re-positioning in its history at Investor Day: unveiling Dragonfly C1000 (250-core server CPU), AI300 accelerator with High Bandwidth Compute Gen2 (54x bandwidth vs prior), securing Meta + Microsoft as anchor hyperscaler customers, acquiring AI software firm Modular for $3.9B, and doubling its FY2029 non-handset revenue target from $22B to $40B.

One-Line Investment Thesis

Qualcomm's unassailable wireless IP moat and on-device AI silicon leadership are the launchpad for a credible edge-to-cloud AI platform — with meaningful optionality from the Dragonfly data-centre push (Meta/Microsoft confirmed) that begins generating revenue in FY2027–28.

Economic Moat Analysis

IP / Patent Moat (QTL)
World's largest 3G/4G/5G SEP portfolio. OEMs cannot ship a 5G device without Qualcomm's licence — enforced by legal victories vs Apple, Huawei, and others. QTL generates ~$8–9B revenue annually at 70%+ operating margins with near-zero incremental cost. This moat is virtually indestructible; it took 30+ years of 3GPP standardization participation to build and cannot be replicated.
★★★★★
Switching Costs (Snapdragon Ecosystem)
Android OEMs design entire product roadmaps around Snapdragon SoCs — 2+ years of chip bring-up, RF tuning, driver rewrites, and carrier certification cycles to switch. Automotive customers locked in even more deeply: 5–7 year vehicle development cycles. Post-Modular, the software toolchain (QNN, AI Hub, Mojo/MAX) adds a second switching-cost layer at the software level.
★★★★
Cost Advantage (Fabless + Scale + R&D)
Fabless model accesses TSMC leading-edge nodes at scale without capex burden. $9B+ annual R&D investment (20%+ of revenue) — impossible for most to match. 45B+ device deployments give optimization data competitors cannot replicate. HBC (High Bandwidth Compute) proprietary memory architecture is a novel cost-performance lever for data-centre inference.
★★★★
Network Effects (Developer Ecosystem)
Snapdragon AI Hub and QNN SDK ecosystem — 10,000s of developers optimizing AI models for Qualcomm NPUs. More developers → better tools → more design wins. Dragonfly + Modular is explicitly designed to create a data-centre equivalent of this loop, challenging NVIDIA CUDA's network effect.
★★★
Efficient Scale (Standards IP Oligopoly)
Only 3–5 companies globally are relevant 5G/6G SEP holders. This is a natural oligopoly enforced by the ITU/3GPP process. New entrants cannot declare themselves standard-essential — patents must be contributed during specification drafting decades earlier. Qualcomm participates in 6G specification now, extending the moat forward.
★★★★★
Intangible Assets (Brand in Automotive)
Snapdragon Digital Chassis is now the reference architecture for connected vehicles. Stellantis, BMW, GM, Renault, Mercedes — all publicly announced. This brand creates an inference loop: "premium vehicles = Snapdragon" → more prestige OEM wins → stronger brand. New OEMs default to Snapdragon to reduce engineering risk.
★★★

  • On-device AI is a structurally distinct compute category: Privacy regulation (GDPR, India DPDP, US state laws), latency requirements (sub-10ms for AR/robotics), and power budgets (mobile battery life) make device inference non-substitutable with cloud.
