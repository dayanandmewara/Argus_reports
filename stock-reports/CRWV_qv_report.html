Business Overview

CoreWeave began in 2017 as Atlantic Crypto, an Ethereum mining operation founded by Michael Intrator, Brian Venturo, Peter Salanki, and Brannin McBee. When crypto mining economics deteriorated, the founders redeployed their GPU fleet toward AI compute — a pivot that positioned them to accumulate NVIDIA hardware allocation years before the AI boom made it scarce. Rebranded CoreWeave in 2019, the company now operates as a specialized "neocloud": it leases and builds data centers, fills them with NVIDIA GPU clusters, and rents that capacity to AI labs and hyperscalers on multi-year, often take-or-pay contracts. Its 2025 acquisitions of Weights & Biases (MLOps/AI developer tooling) and Marimo (open-source Python notebooks) add a software layer on top of raw compute, aimed at building switching costs beyond hardware access alone. A separate attempt to acquire data-center operator Core Scientific for $9B — intended to convert leased capacity into owned capacity — was rejected by Core Scientific's own shareholders in October 2025 and remains unconsummated.

"Price is what you pay, value is what you get." CoreWeave sits at a genuinely rare intersection — mission-critical infrastructure for the defining technology cycle of the decade, wrapped around a balance sheet a value investor would normally run from. The framework's job isn't to resolve that tension for you; it's to make sure you're pricing both halves of it.
Why Not BUY

A BUY verdict requires all four pillars strong, price at/below the MoS entry zone, a confirmed technical trigger, accumulating (not distributing) insider/institutional ownership, and ACCEPTABLE-or-better earnings quality. CRWV clears earnings quality and roughly half of valuation, but Financial Fortress is weak on every quantitative test the framework runs (ROE, ROIC, Altman Z, current ratio, D/E), Moat is contested by the ROIC test and an independent professional research desk, and ownership is actively distributing (>$2.3B in founder sales since Aug 2025) rather than accumulating. None of these are one-quarter problems that a single good earnings print resolves.

Why Not PASS / AVOID

The $99.4B contracted backlog from investment-grade-adjacent counterparties, the NVIDIA backstop through April 2032, Nasdaq-100 inclusion, and a price already down 46% from its 52-week high all argue against dismissing this as low-quality or overpriced. This is a name worth continued, active monitoring — not a business the framework should discard.

B · AI Business Model

CoreWeave doesn't build AI models — it sells the infrastructure everyone else's models run on. Revenue is compute-as-a-service: customers commit to multi-year, often take-or-pay contracts for guaranteed GPU cluster capacity. The Weights & Biases and Marimo acquisitions layer developer tooling (experiment tracking, evaluation, notebooks) on top, aimed at capturing more of the AI workflow than raw compute alone and building switching costs the hardware layer can't provide by itself.
