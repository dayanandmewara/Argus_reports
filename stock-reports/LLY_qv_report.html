Business Overview

Eli Lilly and Company, founded in 1876 and headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana, is the world's most valuable pharmaceutical company by market capitalisation (~$980B as of June 2026). For nearly 150 years, Lilly built a steady but unremarkable compounding record in insulin, oncology, and immunology. Then tirzepatide arrived — and everything changed.

Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, the world's first medicine targeting two distinct gut-hormone pathways simultaneously. Marketed as Mounjaro for type 2 diabetes and Zepbound for obesity, it has redefined cardiometabolic medicine: superior A1C reduction vs semaglutide, 20-22% average body weight loss, and landmark cardioprotective data (SURPASS-CVOT). FY2025 revenue hit $65.2B (+44.7% YoY), and 2026 guidance was set at $80–83B — a trajectory no pharma company has ever achieved at this scale.

Beyond GLP-1, Lilly has a deep pipeline: Orforglipron (oral GLP-1, FDA filing submitted, approval anticipated late 2026), Retatrutide (triple GI agonist for severe obesity), Kisunla (donanemab for Alzheimer's), and Inluriyo (breast cancer). The company has committed $50B+ in US manufacturing expansion since 2020 to meet GLP-1 demand.

Tirzepatide patents provide exclusivity through approximately 2033–2036, with additional protection through manufacturing complexity (no easy biosimilar path for peptide drugs), device patents on the auto-injector, and data exclusivity on SURPASS/SURMOUNT clinical trials. V

$50B+ US manufacturing investment since 2020 is creating a scale advantage that will take competitors 5–7 years to replicate. Peptide API synthesis at GLP-1 volumes requires specialised fill-finish capacity not widely available. Novo's supply constraints in 2023–24 validated this as a structural competitive lever. E

3. Clinical Data Flywheel ★★★★★

SURPASS (T2D), SURMOUNT (obesity), SUMMIT (heart failure), and SURPASS-CVOT represent the deepest cardiometabolic clinical dataset for any incretin. This data creates physician trust, label expansions, and guideline recommendations that are irreplaceable. Tirzepatide's CVOT data confirming cardioprotection is a 10-year generational advantage. V

4. Next-Gen Pipeline Lock-in ★★★★

Orforglipron (oral GLP-1), Retatrutide (triple GI agonist — GLP-1 + GIP + Glucagon), and Efsitora alfa (once-weekly insulin) represent 2–3 pipeline generations ahead of most competitors. If each succeeds, Lilly maintains leadership well into the 2040s. V — Confirmed from Lilly Q4 2025 earnings

5. Switching Cost / Physician Inertia ★★★

Once a physician establishes a GLP-1 prescribing pattern (titration protocol, auto-injector training, patient education), switching to a new molecule involves clinical and administrative friction. Tirzepatide's superior efficacy data creates a "best-in-class" anchor in prescriber behaviour. Moderate switching cost, reinforced by pharmacy benefit manager contracts. E

6. Brand / Habit ★★

GLP-1 is increasingly a consumer-recognised category (Mounjaro/Zepbound brand awareness high). However, this is not yet a Buffett-style "consumer habit" moat — brand switching if oral formulations deliver similar efficacy with lower cost is a real risk (Novo Nordisk oral semaglutide approved Dec 2025). Q1: What is the proprietary training data?
Lilly possesses arguably the world's most extensive GLP-1 clinical outcomes dataset. The SURPASS programme (6 Phase 3 trials, T2D) and SURMOUNT programme (4 Phase 3 trials, obesity) cumulatively covered 100,000+ patient-years of tirzepatide outcomes. SUMMIT (heart failure) and SURPASS-CVOT add cardiovascular outcomes data. Real-world data from LillyDirect (direct-to-patient platform) adds adherence, dosing titration, and side-effect profiles not available to competitors. Volume: ★★★★★. Uniqueness: ★★★★★. Outcome labels: ★★★★★ (weight reduction %, A1C, cardiac events — ideal for supervised learning).
Q2: How does AI improve with more users?
The NVIDIA TuneLab collaboration applies patient data to molecular optimisation — more patient response data allows better prediction of which molecular modifications improve efficacy or reduce GI side effects. LillyDirect adherence data enables AI to personalise dosing escalation schedules, predicting which patients tolerate faster titration (improving therapeutic outcomes). Insilico Medicine's AI platform is being trained on Lilly's proprietary target + clinical data, compressing hit-to-lead timelines.
Q3: Does better AI attract more users?
NOT YET CONFIRMED. The hypothesised loop: Better AI dosing → Better patient outcomes → More physician/patient referrals → More LillyDirect data → Even better AI. This loop is theoretically valid but not yet independently documented at measurable scale. LillyDirect is live and growing but specific patient acquisition data attributable to AI improvements is not yet disclosed in public filings. Watch LillyDirect user metrics in Q2/Q3 2026 earnings.
