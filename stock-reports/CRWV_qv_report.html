Business Overview

CoreWeave (NASDAQ: CRWV), founded in 2017, is America's premier specialised GPU cloud provider — the "neocloud" that powers the AI models being built by OpenAI, Meta, Anthropic, Microsoft, and CrowdStrike. The company began as an Ethereum mining operation called Atlantic Crypto, pivoted to GPU compute in 2019, and exploded into AI infrastructure as generative AI demand surged in 2022–2023.

CoreWeave is not a software company. It is a capital infrastructure business with software-grade gross margins — a rare and important distinction. It buys NVIDIA's most advanced GPUs, builds purpose-built data centres, and rents GPU compute at scale to AI labs that need massive training clusters and inference infrastructure. Think of it as an electricity utility for the AI economy: essential, high-volume, capital-intensive, and increasingly indispensable.

VERIFIED As of December 31, 2025: 43 active data centres, 850MW active power capacity, 3.1GW contracted, $66.8B revenue backlog (4x YoY growth), 2,190 employees. The company is the sole SemiAnalysis Platinum ClusterMAX™ provider and the first NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud for GB200 NVL72. VERIFIED

Why This Stock is in the Series

CoreWeave represents the most capital-intensive bet on the AI infrastructure build-out currently available to public market investors. It sits at Level 1 of the AI Value Chain — infrastructure that wins regardless of which foundation model ultimately dominates. The $66.8B revenue backlog (13x FY2025 revenue) provides exceptional forward visibility rarely seen in public markets. However, the $30B+ FY2026 CapEx commitment on a $5.1B revenue base is an extraordinary capital gamble. The Buffett framework must be heavily adapted for this asset: standard DCF, ROIC, and earnings quality tests are irrelevant. The relevant questions are: (1) Is the NVIDIA moat durable? (2) Is the customer concentration risk being addressed? (3) Can the gross margin structure survive hyperscaler competition?

🌀 DATA FLYWHEEL ANALYSIS — CoreWeave
Question 1: Does CoreWeave have proprietary training data competitors cannot access?

PARTIAL. CoreWeave observes GPU utilisation patterns, job scheduling data, inference vs. training workload ratios, and cluster performance metrics across 850MW of compute. This operational data informs Mission Control optimisation. However, this is operational telemetry — not the kind of outcome-labelled training data (like HDFC Bank's default history or Naukri's hiring outcomes) that creates a deep ML flywheel. The data moat is real but narrow. ESTIMATED

Question 2: Does the AI improve measurably as more data/users are added?

FORMING. Mission Control's GPU straggler detection, telemetry relay, and cluster optimisation improve with scale. The company can place workloads more efficiently with experience. However, unlike pure software flywheels, the AI improvement here is primarily operational efficiency, not a product intelligence that compounds exponentially. ESTIMATED

Question 3: Does better AI attract more users, creating the feedback loop?

LIMITED. AI labs choose CoreWeave primarily for: (1) NVIDIA access, (2) power capacity, (3) contract terms — not primarily for Mission Control's AI intelligence. The flywheel is forming but the primary moat is supply-chain and infrastructure scale, not a data-driven AI feedback loop. The W&B acquisition adds a genuine developer flywheel (more experiments → better platform → more experiments) but this is a developing asset. ESTIMATED
