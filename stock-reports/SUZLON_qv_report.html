Investment Thesis

Deleveraged balance sheet and booming wind order book provide strong turnaround momentum, but valuation remains rich and cyclical execution risks persist.

Why This Stock is Interesting

Suzlon is a premier case of a successful financial turnaround. Reaching a net-cash position of ₹1,943 Cr in FY25, it has shed legacy international liabilities and refocused on the booming Indian wind energy cycle, driven by massive government execution mandates.

Key Structural Risks

  • Wind turbine business remains cyclical and policy-sensitive.
  • Competitive bidding pressures from global and local rivals.
  • Low promoter holding (11.74% in Mar 2025).

Series Rationale

The Buffett Quality-Value Series analyzes companies through the lens of long-term capital efficiency, economic moats, and financial stability. Suzlon's deleveraged balance sheet makes it a viable candidate for evaluation, though the cyclical WTG manufacturing sector violates several traditional "Buffett-style" consumer franchise moats. This report focuses on whether the high-margin O&M business provides a durable annuity stream capable of acting as an economic cushion.
