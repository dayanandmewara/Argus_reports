Analyst Note

Lodha Developers (renamed from Macrotech Developers in June 2025) is India's largest listed residential developer by sales value, and we slot it into Category C — Mature Quality Compounder: ROCE has climbed from a 5-year average near 10% to 15.75% today, revenue and profit are compounding well above the sector's base growth rate, but the business remains capital-intensive and cyclical, ruling out Category A/B treatment. The AI Overlay is NEUTRAL — the company runs conventional CRM/ABM and facility-management digitisation (BelleVie resident app, 6sense marketing stack) with no proprietary data flywheel; AI here is an efficiency tool, not a moat lever, consistent with the sector-wide NEUTRAL rating. Macro relevance is real: a 6.6-6.9% GDP growth path, a neutral RBI rate stance at a 5.25% repo rate, and a still-low CPI print all support urban housing affordability, even as a downgraded FY27 GDP forecast injects some caution. What makes this stock interesting is the combination of a record FY26 (PAT +24%, margins crossing 20% for the first time, zero promoter pledge, net debt/equity at 0.23x against a self-imposed 0.5x ceiling) with a stock price that has fallen well below its 200-day moving average. What makes it challenging is that trailing P/E of ~31x and a negative earnings-yield spread to the 10-year G-Sec leave little room for disappointment — and FY26's own pre-sales guidance was missed by 2% on an external shock. One framework adaptation is applied throughout: Owner-Earnings-Capitalisation and ROIC/EPV (Methods 2-3) structurally understate a developer sitting on a ₹2 lakh crore secured land bank, since they capitalise one year of earnings in perpetuity — we flag this explicitly in Tab 5 rather than let it silently anchor the blended intrinsic value.

Business Overview

VERIFIED Lodha Developers Ltd (formerly Macrotech Developers Ltd; renamed June 2025) is a Mumbai-headquartered real estate developer led by MD & CEO Abhishek Lodha, listed on NSE/BSE since its April 2021 IPO. The group's core business is residential development — luxury, premium and mid-income housing — concentrated in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR), with a scaling presence in Pune and Bengaluru and a new FY26 entry into Delhi-NCR. A smaller but growing logistics/industrial-parks arm (Lodha Industrial & Logistics) and an emerging commercial/retail annuity portfolio sit alongside the residential core.

VERIFIED FY26 highlights: revenue ₹16,676 Cr (+21% YoY), net profit ₹3,431 Cr (+24% YoY, first year of >20% PAT margin), pre-sales ₹20,530 Cr (+16% YoY, though below the company's own ₹21,000 Cr guidance due to West-Asia-conflict-linked customer deferments), and a secured land bank with ₹2 lakh crore of Gross Development Value (GDV) available for sale.

Framework Adaptation — Read This First

Real estate developers break the standard perpetuity-based methods. Methods 2 (Owner Earnings Capitalisation) and 3 (ROIC/EPV) capitalise a single year of earnings into perpetuity — a reasonable approach for a steady-state compounder, but one that structurally understates a business sitting on a ₹2 lakh crore secured, multi-year land bank that is not yet reflected in trailing earnings. Method 1 (DCF), which explicitly projects the multi-year monetisation of that pipeline, is the more appropriate primary method here; Methods 2–3 are shown as a conservative floor, not a fair-value estimate. Street NAV/SOTP-based targets (Tab 5 bottom) sit well above all four quantitative methods for the same reason, in reverse — they capture the land-bank optionality that a pure earnings-based DCF discounts heavily.
