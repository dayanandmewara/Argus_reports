Business Overview

ABB India Limited, incorporated in 1949, is a 75%-owned subsidiary of ABB Ltd (Switzerland), one of the world's largest power and automation technology groups. Operating for 76 years in India, ABB India serves 23 industry segments across four core business areas: Electrification (switchgear, circuit breakers, UPS, low-voltage products), Motion (drives, motors, generators), Process Automation (DCS, SCADA, measurement analytics), and Robotics & Discrete Automation (industrial robots, welding systems, machine automation). ~85% of products sold in India are locally manufactured across 9 plants and 23 offices.

Why ABB India Is in This Series

ABB India is the canonical example of a world-class quality business priced at a world-class premium — a recurring phenomenon in Indian capital goods. The government's ₹12.2 lakh crore capex supercycle directly feeds ABB's order book. ROIC has been 50–115% over the past 5 years — almost Category A quality in Category C clothing. But FY2025 delivered a warning: revenue growth slowed to 8% while net profit fell 11%, flagging rising import costs, QCO compliance headwinds, and competition. At 77× P/E vs India's 10-year G-sec yield of 6.9%, the earnings yield of 1.3% means ABB India has a deeply negative equity risk premium. The series verdict: exceptional franchise, patient investor's target, wrong price right now.

Economic Moat Analysis
🏭 Installed Base & Switching Costs★★★★★
ABB India has the deepest installed base of automation and electrification equipment in Indian industry. Once ABB drives, SCADA, or DCS are commissioned in a plant, switching costs are enormous — retraining engineers, recertification, process downtime. Industrial customers on ABB Ability digital subscriptions face increasing switching costs as historical data accumulates. Status: STRENGTHENS in any macro scenario.
🌐 Global Technology Parent (ABB Ltd)★★★★★
ABB India uniquely benefits from ABB Ltd's global R&D (Switzerland). Technology, IP, and product development flows freely into ABB India at no explicit cost — a structural advantage no Indian competitor can replicate. ABB globally invested billions in R&D per year, of which ABB India benefits without bearing the cost burden. Additionally, ABB Ltd's $75M direct investment in India manufacturing in 2026 shows commitment. Status: HOLDS — dependent on parent's health (which is strong).
🏆 Brand & Technology Reputation★★★★
ABB is one of the most trusted brands in industrial automation globally. In India, ABB's 76-year track record, certification credentials, and government/PSU relationships are deeply entrenched. For mission-critical applications (railways, power grids, chemical plants), brand trust is a primary selection criterion. Status: HOLDS — brand premium unlikely to erode.
⚡ Efficient Scale / Local Manufacturing★★★
~85% local manufacturing across 9 plants gives ABB India a cost advantage vs pure importers and manufacturing scale vs smaller domestic competitors. Government's "Make in India" and localization mandates further reinforce this position. However, QCO compliance has temporarily elevated import costs — showing this moat is not absolute. Status: HOLDS with pressure — QCO headwind is near-term.
🔗 Government/PSU Relationships★★★★
Railways (Titagarh Rail Systems propulsion deal for metro), power utilities, ONGC, HPCL, SAIL — ABB India has long-standing relationships with India's most important public sector customers. These are sticky, often multi-decade relationships that require immense institutional trust to dislodge. Status: STRENGTHENS — government capex supercycle directly flows here.
