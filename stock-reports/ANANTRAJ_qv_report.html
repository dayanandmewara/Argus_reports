Business Overview

Anant Raj Limited, founded in 1969 and listed since 2006, is one of Delhi-NCR's most storied real estate developers — and is now executing a deliberate transformation into India's AI-compute infrastructure backbone. The company has developed over 9.96 msf of real estate across residential townships, commercial complexes, IT parks, hospitality, and affordable housing, primarily in Delhi, Haryana, and NCR.

What makes Anant Raj uniquely interesting in this series is its dual-engine model: legacy real estate generates strong and growing cash flows (₹2,060 Cr revenue in FY25, growing at ~20% YoY), while the emerging data center business — housed under subsidiary Anant Raj Cloud (ARCPL) — is positioned to capture India's explosive AI-driven data center demand. The company's 320 acres of debt-free NCR land is a strategic moat for data center development: land acquisition is the single biggest bottleneck for data center expansion in India today.

As Managing Director Amit Sarin puts it: "India generates 28% of the world's data but houses only 1% of it locally — this is an inevitable correction." ESTIMATED The company is now operationalizing 28 MW of data center capacity (Manesar + Panchkula), targeting 63 MW by Dec 2026, 117 MW by FY28, and an ambitious 307 MW by FY32 — backed by a USD 2.1 billion capex plan.

Investment Thesis (One Paragraph)

The Soil-to-Server Compounding Story: Anant Raj is using its heritage real estate franchise — generating ₹2,000+ Cr/year in revenue with improving margins — to self-fund a capital-intensive pivot into India's data center infrastructure boom. With 320 acres of debt-free NCR land (India's most constrained input for DC development), proprietary colocation + cloud services bundled under Anant Raj Cloud, and institutional backing from global investors post-QIP (Société Générale, Morgan Stanley, Jupiter India Fund), the company is positioning itself as India's best-value data center developer. The thesis requires management execution on a 10x data center revenue ramp over 6 years — this is the central risk and central opportunity. At ₹504, the stock prices in ~70% of this execution. Entry at ₹400–440 would offer adequate margin of safety for the base case.
