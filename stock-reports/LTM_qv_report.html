LTM Limited (formerly LTIMindtree, rebranded March 2026) is the Larsen & Toubro Group's technology services subsidiary and India's sixth-largest IT services firm. Formed via the 2022 merger of L&T Infotech and Mindtree, LTM serves 751 active clients across BFSI (33%), Technology Media & Comms (25%), Manufacturing (20%), Consumer (15%), and Healthcare (7%), delivering cloud transformation, data analytics, enterprise applications, cybersecurity, and AI-led digital solutions. In FY26, the company generated $4.76 billion (~₹43,402 Cr) in revenue at EBIT margin of 15.4%, with $6.60 billion in order inflow — the highest in company history.

This analysis is Category C — a Mature Quality Compounder with ROIC estimated 22–25%, durable switching-cost moat via multi-year client contracts and deep enterprise integration, and stable growth of 7–9% in dollar terms. It is NOT Category A (no consumer monopoly characteristics; ROIC does not consistently exceed 40%) nor Category B (revenue growth well below 25% CAGR). The framework applies standard 5-method DCF + Owner Earnings + EPV + P/E + Earnings Yield valuation, WACC 12–13%, terminal P/E 18–22x.

The 🔴 AI THREAT overlay is the central tension of this analysis. The Indian IT services sector is classified THREAT in the framework: AI-led productivity sharing is actively eroding revenue per engineer, OpenAI's deployment company launch (May 2026) threatens direct competition with traditional IT consulting, and analyst consensus is forecasting AI deflation headwinds for FY27 and FY28. LTM's BlueVerse™ ecosystem is a credible — but early — response. We apply a 15% IV discount and shorten investment horizon to 3–5 years. The key bet: does BlueVerse™ pivot LTM from AI THREAT to AI NEUTRAL/WIDENER before deflation compounds?

Why This Stock Is Interesting & Challenging

Interesting: LTM is trading at a 38% discount to its 52-week high amid widespread sector de-rating — making it superficially cheap. The company has the strongest order book in its history ($6.6B), the L&T pedigree, zero meaningful debt, and a credible AI pivot underway (BlueVerse™ Credit, agentic AI studios). If the AI transition succeeds in repositioning LTM from labour-arbitrage IT services to outcome-based AI delivery, re-rating potential is significant.

Challenging: AI deflation is structural, not cyclical. AI coding agents, OpenAI's deployment company, and Anthropic automation tools are compressing IT services revenue per headcount industry-wide. LTM's 3-year EPS CAGR is barely 4% despite double-digit revenue growth — margins are under structural pressure. Mutual fund holdings have declined 105 bps over three quarters, signalling institutional patience wearing thin. This is a company in genuine transformation — outcome uncertain, with re-rating risk in both directions.
