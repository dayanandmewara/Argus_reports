Business Overview

Super Micro Computer (SMCI) designs and manufactures application-optimised server and storage solutions purpose-built for AI, cloud, edge, and enterprise workloads. Founded in 1993 by Charles Liang in San Jose, it has evolved from a motherboard specialist into a full-stack data-centre infrastructure provider. Its "Building Block" philosophy — modular, open-architecture components combining CPUs, GPUs, memory, storage, and cooling — enables customised rack-scale deployments at speed.

SMCI's differentiated edge is its Direct Liquid Cooling (DLC) technology. As NVIDIA's Blackwell/Rubin GPUs push thermal envelopes beyond what air cooling can handle, SMCI's capacity of 3,000 DLC racks/month (total capacity 6,000 racks/month) positions it as the preferred integrator. Its ability to design, qualify, and ship GPU servers within 6 weeks of a new chip launch is structurally faster than rivals Dell, HPE, and Lenovo. ESTIMATED

Revenue has compounded at >65% annually over the past 3 years — driven entirely by AI infrastructure capex from hyperscalers, Neo-clouds, CSPs, and sovereign AI programs — reaching $22B in FY2025 and tracking toward $40B in FY2026. SMCI commands approximately 10% of the global AI server market. VERIFIED

⚠ CRITICAL GOVERNANCE RISK — READ BEFORE PROCEEDING

The company itself is NOT indicted and is cooperating. A securities class action has been filed (class period April 2024–March 2026). Prior to this: Ernst & Young resigned as auditor in 2024; BDO now auditing; material weaknesses in internal controls disclosed. The DOJ event caused a 33.3% single-day stock drop on March 20, 2026.
