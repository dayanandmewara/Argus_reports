Business Overview

Websol Energy System Limited is one of India's oldest and largest standalone solar photovoltaic cell manufacturers, with a continuous manufacturing history dating to 1994. The company operates a world-class facility at Falta Special Economic Zone (SEZ), West Bengal, with 1.2 GW Mono PERC Bifacial solar cell capacity (doubled in September 2025) and 550 MW solar module capacity. Websol supplies cells primarily to Indian module manufacturers for DCR compliance, while its modules reach both domestic and international markets. The company is now upgrading one cell line to TOPCon technology (>24.5% efficiency target, total capacity to 1.35 GW) and has secured government approval for a 4 GW greenfield facility in Andhra Pradesh (target: June 2027 commissioning). CRISIL rating: BBB+/Stable.

Order Book: ₹1,161 Cr Cell Util: >90% Module Util: 74% BBB+/Stable First Dividend FY26: ₹0.25/sh
Why Interesting — The ALMM Catalyst

ALMM List-II (effective June 2026) mandates that module makers sourcing cells for Indian government projects must use domestically ALMM-listed cell producers. Websol is one of <10 listed domestic cell manufacturers. This creates a regulatory moat — essentially guaranteed demand flow from 119.8 GW of listed module manufacturers. Combined with 40% BCD on modules and 25% on cells, Websol operates in a structurally protected environment with pricing power unusual for a commodity manufacturer.

Why Challenging — The Framework Problem

Solar cells are fundamentally commodity products. The ALMM moat is regulatory, not structural — historically, ALMM has been suspended and reinstated, and policy reversal risk is real. More critically, the promoter has pledged 89.4% of their holding — a forced-sale scenario could wipe out significant equity value. The Buffett framework applies partially: Cat D turnaround with extreme caution. Buffett would almost certainly not invest with this pledging profile.

Series Rationale & Framework Adaptation

Websol is a Cat D — Turnaround/Special Situation stock. The company was essentially non-operational in FY24 (₹26 Cr revenue) while upgrading to 600 MW Mono PERC Bifacial technology. FY25 marked restart; FY26 confirmed the business model. WACC 14–16%, terminal P/E 15–18×, MoS 25%. Framework deviation: given commodity-like solar cell pricing, we apply DCF on Owner Earnings (not revenue), and run all 5 methods with high weight on conservative scenarios. Regulatory / ALMM Moat
★★★★

Websol is one of the few listed producers with operational scale. BCD 25% on cells + 40% on modules create pricing protection vs Chinese imports.
