🏢 Business Overview
Company Identity

KEI Industries Limited (formerly Krishna Electrical Industries, est. 1968) is India's second-largest cables and wires manufacturer by revenue, trailing only Polycab India. The company operates across three segments: Cables & Wires (~96% of revenue), EPC Services (~4%), and Stainless Steel Wire (residual). With 9 manufacturing plants, 2,082 dealers, and exports to 55+ countries, KEI is a genuine all-voltage manufacturer — the only Indian company qualified in National Grid UK's framework for cables up to 400 kV. Moat Thesis — Narrow to Mid Width

KEI's primary moat is its EHV capability. Being one of a handful of global manufacturers qualified for 400kV cables — and the only Indian company qualified under the National Grid UK framework — creates genuine pricing power and project exclusivity in the EHV segment (717 Cr of the ₹3,928 Cr order book). The brand + dealer network in B2C house wires is a meaningful secondary moat: 30,000+ channel partners, established retail brand, and BIS compliance create credibility that cheaper unorganized players cannot match. However, in standard LT/HT cables (commodity), KEI competes on price — minimal moat. Overall verdict: B moat — real but narrow; dependent on continued capex investment to sustain.

🛡️ Section E — AI Disruption Risk Assessment
AI Threat Assessment — LOW

Cable and wire manufacturing is a physically-intensive, materials-based business. AI cannot replace copper drawing, extrusion, insulation, and cable-laying processes. The product is a physical commodity with precise electrical specifications — no digital substitution possible. AI disruption timeline: Remote (10+ years) at most. Moat Resilience Score against AI disruption: 9/10. The cable industry is arguably one of the MOST AI-resistant businesses in India's industrial sector.

This is a feature, not a bug. KEI's AI-resistance means its economic model will not be disrupted by foundation model improvements or generative AI — unlike IT services or content businesses. The electrification cycle is AI-resistant; it may even be AI-accelerated (data centres need cables).
