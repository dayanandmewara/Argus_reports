Business Overview
The Aurora Powerhouse

Fast-fission, sodium-cooled compact reactor modeled on EBR-II heritage. Output: 15–75 MWe per unit, scalable to 100+ MWe in modular configuration. Runs on HALEU fuel or recycled used nuclear fuel. 10-year fuel cycle without refueling. Passive cooling = meltdown-proof design. 205 employees. First deployment: INL, Idaho.

Three Business Units

Power: Build-Own-Operate model; long-term PPAs with data centers, industrials, defense.
Fuel: Aurora Fuel Fabrication Facility (A3F) at INL recycles EBR-II spent fuel — first HALEU fuel recycler in the US.
Isotopes: Atomic Alchemy acquisition enables medical/industrial radioisotope revenue stream from reactor byproducts.

Economic Moat Assessment — Grade: C+

Oklo possesses an embryonic but potentially wide moat rooted in proprietary fast-fission IP, 60+ years of EBR-II operational heritage data, first-mover advantage in US advanced fast fission, and a vertically integrated build-own-operate model. The moat is not yet commercially proven — it upgrades to B+/A- if INL deployment executes on schedule and first PPA revenue is recognized. Current grade C+ reflects pre-commercial status; structural moat sources are real and widening.

Switching Costs ★★★★

Long-term PPAs (20-30 years with Air Force Eielson, multi-decade with hyperscalers) create massive switching costs. Once a data center is co-located and infrastructure-integrated with Aurora power delivery, migrating to another source requires facility redesign, permitting, and years of lead time. Equinix's $25M prepayment is a structural lock-in signal. VFD

Proprietary Technology IP ★★★★

Fast-fission sodium-cooled design based on EBR-II (world's most successful experimental breeder reactor, operated 1964-1994). Oklo holds exclusive access to EBR-II operational data and fuel heritage. The Aurora design, fuel recycling process, and HALEU fabrication technology represent unique IP that cannot be replicated overnight. Kiewit EPC engagement further embeds proprietary construction knowledge.

Vertical Integration ★★★★★

Oklo is the ONLY advanced SMR developer pursuing full vertical integration: reactor design + fuel fabrication + fuel recycling + power generation + isotope production. Competitors (NuScale, X-Energy, Kairos) are reactor-only, requiring external fuel supply chains. Oklo's vertical model provides: (1) fuel cost certainty, (2) waste stream monetization, (3) margin capture across all layers, (4) proprietary operational data at every stage. EXCEPTIONAL DIFFERENTIATOR

Network Effects / AI Pull ★★★

Limited traditional network effects (power is not a platform). However, the AI data center demand cluster creates a powerful customer concentration effect: as Oklo signs Meta, Switch, Equinix — other hyperscalers feel pressure to secure reliable nuclear power, creating a demand network effect. NVIDIA partnership for AI-nuclear research creates a technology credibility loop with the AI sector.

Intangible Assets / Regulatory Position ★★★★

NRC engagement history, DOE relationship, INL site-use permit (granted 2019), participation in Reactor Pilot Program, Surplus Plutonium Utilization Program selection, HALEU access rights from EBR-II — these are non-replicable regulatory positions. No other advanced SMR developer has an active build at a DOE national laboratory site. New entrants face 5-10 year regulatory lag.

Scale / Cost Advantage (Forming) ★★

Not yet achieved — first reactor is under construction. The promise is factory-built modular standardization (PBOL regulatory framework) enabling cost-curve improvement with each subsequent Aurora unit (similar to how solar $/W declined with scale). Cost advantage moat grade upgrades to ★★★★ if the factory-build replication thesis is demonstrated by reactor 3-5.
