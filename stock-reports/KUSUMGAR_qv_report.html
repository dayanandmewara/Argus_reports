Business Overview VERIFIED

Incorporated in 1990, Kusumgar Limited manufactures woven, coated and laminated engineered synthetic fabrics — high-performance textiles built on polyamide and polyester filaments with polyurethane chemistry, designed for tensile strength, waterproofing, flame resistance and abrasion resistance rather than fashion. The company has developed over 1,000 fabric SKUs across four segments: Aerospace & Defence Fabrics, Aerospace & Defence Solutions (finished products — parachute systems, camouflage, ballistic and rapid-deployment materials), Industrial & Automotive Fabrics, and Outdoor & Lifestyle Fabrics (its fabricator network supplies brands including Decathlon). Manufacturing runs across six facilities in Gujarat plus one fabrication unit in Uttar Pradesh, employing roughly 2,077 people including contract staff. Kusumgar holds technical/licensing partnerships with companies in the US, Italy, Japan, Switzerland and Taiwan covering parachute systems, camouflage fabrics, ballistic materials and custom yarn development.

Why Category D — Special Situation

Kusumgar doesn't fit neatly into a steady-compounder box. It has a real moat component — aerospace/defence customer qualification cycles that reportedly run two to ten years, which is a genuine switching-cost barrier once a customer is won. But the P&L behind that moat is lumpy: a single ₹205 crore order drove much of FY25's aerospace & defence fabric revenue, and its absence pulled that segment down ~42% in FY26. Combined with a fresh listing, weak cash conversion, and a capital structure event (100% Offer for Sale, no fresh capital to the company), this reads as an event-driven special situation rather than a "buy and hold for a decade" compounder — hence Category D, with a correspondingly higher WACC (14–16%) and larger margin of safety (25%) than a Category A/C name would get.

What's interesting

Real, hard-to-copy entry barriers in a defence-adjacent niche; 60% gross margin and industry-leading 27%+ EBITDA margin even in a down revenue year; a well-subscribed-looking anchor book (₹193.95 Cr from 23 institutions including Nippon India MF, SBI MF, Blackrock and Goldman Sachs) VERIFIED; and promoters retaining an estimated ~76% stake even after the OFS — this is not a full promoter exit.

What's challenging

FY26 revenue and PAT both fell (-11%/-12%) right into the IPO window; operating cash flow conversion collapsed to ~29% of PAT on a receivables blowout; total debt rose sharply since FY24; a loan covenant was breached in FY25 (subsequently waived); customer concentration is high (top 10 = ~60% of revenue); and — critically — none of the ₹650 Cr raised goes to the company, so none of it funds the newly tripled manufacturing capacity currently running under 50% utilisation.

Moat Threats

Raw material supply risk (no long-term nylon/polyester supply agreements disclosed); geographic concentration (all six plants in Gujarat); tariff exposure on the ~10%-of-revenue US export book; and the risk that "growth" segments (outdoor/lifestyle, industrial/auto) that offset the FY26 defence decline are lower-barrier, more competitive categories than the core defence fabric business.

Strengths

Promoter family (Kusumgar) has run the business since 1990 with three decades of sector-specific expertise. Despite selling ₹650 Cr worth of shares via OFS, the promoter group is estimated to retain roughly 76% of the company post-listing — this is a partial monetisation, not an exit. International co-development partnerships suggest credible technical relationships rather than a purely opportunistic listing.

Concerns

The IPO is 100% Offer for Sale — every rupee goes to the promoter selling shareholders, none to the company, even as the business just took on debt to triple processing capacity that's running under 50% utilised. A financial covenant on a ₹100 Cr loan was breached in FY25 (subsequently waived by the lender) — worth monitoring, not disqualifying on its own. Working capital deterioration (90 days vs. 14 days) occurred in the same year management chose to list and cash out partially.
