Company Transformation Story

Nebius Group N.V. is the most dramatic corporate transformation in the BQV series. Once known as Yandex N.V. — Russia's dominant search and internet conglomerate — the company underwent a geopolitical forced metamorphosis following Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine. By August 2024, the Russian assets were sold and the rump holding was rechristened Nebius: a clean-slate, Netherlands-domiciled, NASDAQ-listed AI infrastructure pure-play.

What emerged is structurally rare: a company with deep engineering DNA (Yandex had world-class machine learning and distributed systems teams), access to the West's capital markets, NVIDIA preferred-partner status, and a fully owned global data center footprint — without the regulatory overhang of US hyperscalers or the sanction risk of its predecessor. It is not a startup. It is a seasoned technology team building the infrastructure layer for the AI economy.

Why This Stock Is In The BQV Series

Nebius represents the BQV series' first pure infrastructure play in the AI value chain — distinct from the AI-enabled incumbents (HDFC Bank, Naukri) and AI-native software plays (Newgen) analysed previously. It answers a specific investment question: can an independent, vertically integrated GPU cloud provider carve a durable position between commodity cloud rental and hyperscaler self-ownership?

The evidence so far is compelling — 684% revenue growth, 74% gross margin, positive adj EBITDA at 45% in the core business, sold-out capacity, and $50B+ in committed customer contracts — but the risk profile is equally dramatic: $20–25B in annual capex, negative adj net income, massive execution dependency, and a stock price that has run 134% YTD to an ATH. This is a high-conviction Phase 2→3 inflection play that requires patience and a specific entry discipline.
